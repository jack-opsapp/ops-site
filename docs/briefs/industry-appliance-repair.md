## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- The U.S. appliance repair industry is a **$7.0 billion market** with **37,769 businesses**, growing at a CAGR of 2.5% over the past five years. The market is highly fragmented -- the top 4 companies hold only 6.9% market share, leaving the vast majority to independent operators and small companies.
- Appliance repair has a **unique operational complexity**: technicians must diagnose problems on-site, determine if they have the right parts on their truck, and either complete the repair or schedule a return visit. The industry average **first-time fix rate is only 75%**, meaning 1 in 4 jobs requires a costly second truck roll.
- **Parts inventory management is the #1 differentiator** between profitable and unprofitable appliance repair companies. Ensuring technicians carry the right parts eliminates return trips, reduces fuel costs, and increases daily job capacity. Yet most FSM platforms treat inventory as a generic afterthought.
- The competitive landscape includes Workiz (popular for small-mid appliance repair), Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan ($245-500/tech/mo), and FieldEdge. None offer appliance-specific part lookup, warranty tracking integration, or first-time fix rate analytics as core features.
- OPS has a clear opening: flat $79/mo pricing, mobile-first job documentation with photo capture for diagnostics, offline capability (basements and utility rooms), and crew management that works for a 2-tech operation or a 15-van fleet.

## Industry Profile

### Market Size
- U.S. appliance repair industry: **$7.0 billion** (2025-2026)
- **37,769 businesses** in the United States
- Industry revenue CAGR: **2.5%** over the past five years
- Global market: **$12.28-$14.9 billion** (2025-2026)
- Top 4 companies hold only **6.9% market share** -- extremely fragmented
- Average sales per location: **$0.9 million**

### Industry Segments
- **Solo operator / mobile technician:** 1-2 people, one van, $80K-$200K revenue
- **Small company:** 2-5 technicians, 2-4 vans, $200K-$600K revenue
- **Established operation:** 5-15 technicians, 5-10 vans, $600K-$2M+ revenue
- **Franchise operations:** Mr. Appliance, Sears Home Services, regional chains
- **Warranty service providers:** Authorized service for manufacturers (Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, GE)

### Service Pricing & Revenue Mix
- Service/diagnostic call: $80-$150
- Average repair cost: $150-$400 (varies by appliance)
- Revenue breakdown: **60-80% labor and diagnostic fees, 20-40% parts sales**
- Refrigerator repair: $200-$400
- Washer/dryer repair: $150-$350
- Dishwasher repair: $150-$300
- Oven/range repair: $150-$400
- HVAC appliance repair: $200-$500+

### Technology Adoption
- Industry transitioning from paper-based dispatch to digital, but slowly
- Manufacturers increasingly require digital documentation for warranty claims
- Customer expectations have shifted: 24/7 online booking, text updates, accurate arrival windows, digital invoices
- Most small operations still use phone-based scheduling, paper invoices, and spreadsheets for parts tracking
- Larger operations use Workiz, Housecall Pro, or ServiceTitan

### Key Operational Metrics
- **First-time fix rate industry average: 75%** -- improving to 90%+ dramatically increases profitability
- Second truck rolls cost $150-$300 in wasted labor and fuel per callback
- Parts availability on the truck is the #1 driver of first-time fix rate
- Average technician handles 4-8 service calls per day depending on complexity

### Labor Situation
- Skilled appliance repair technicians increasingly difficult to recruit
- Modern appliances (smart appliances, electronic controls) require specialized training
- Many technicians are aging out -- younger workers drawn to higher-paying HVAC/electrical trades
- Manufacturer certifications (Samsung, LG, Whirlpool) required for warranty work

## Pain Points (3 Blocks)

### PAIN POINT 1: ONE IN FOUR JOBS REQUIRES A SECOND TRIP -- AND EACH ONE COSTS YOU $200+
**forLine:** "For appliance repair owners watching profits disappear into second truck rolls because technicians didn't have the right parts"

**Bullets:**
1. **The industry average first-time fix rate is only 75%.** That means 1 in 4 service calls requires a return visit because the technician couldn't complete the repair on-site. Each callback costs $150-$300 in wasted labor, fuel, and lost capacity for new jobs.
2. **Parts availability on the truck determines profitability.** When a technician diagnoses a failed water inlet valve but doesn't have one on the van, the customer waits days for the part to arrive, ties up a future appointment slot, and might call a competitor in the meantime.
3. **Tracking hundreds of parts across multiple vans is chaos without a system.** Refrigerator compressor, washer pump, dryer heating element, dishwasher spray arm -- every appliance brand uses different part numbers. Knowing what's on which truck requires real-time inventory visibility.
4. **Improving first-time fix rate from 75% to 90% directly increases revenue.** Fewer callbacks means more capacity for new billable jobs, lower fuel costs, and higher customer satisfaction scores that drive referrals and reviews.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Improving FTFR from 75% to 90%+ directly boosts profit by reducing costly callbacks
- Ensuring technicians have common parts on hand completes repairs faster
- Second truck rolls waste fuel, labor, and customer goodwill

### PAIN POINT 2: CUSTOMERS EXPECT AMAZON-LEVEL SERVICE FROM A 3-PERSON SHOP
**forLine:** "For appliance repair companies losing jobs because they can't match the booking experience customers get from Sears and franchise chains"

**Bullets:**
1. **Homeowners expect 24/7 online booking, text updates, and accurate arrival windows.** They're comparing your service to Amazon delivery tracking, not to the local repair shop that answers the phone at 9 AM.
2. **Manufacturers require precise digital documentation for warranty payouts.** Model numbers, serial numbers, failure codes, photos of the defect, parts used -- paper forms don't cut it anymore, and incomplete documentation means denied warranty claims.
3. **Customer calls and jobs get lost in the chaos.** Without centralized scheduling and dispatch, quotes don't get sent in time, follow-ups fall through the cracks, and customers who called three days ago still haven't been scheduled.
4. **The franchise chains have technology advantages.** Sears Home Services, Mr. Appliance, and authorized dealer networks use enterprise platforms that provide real-time ETAs, automated reminders, and professional digital invoices. Independent shops need the same capabilities without the franchise overhead.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Homeowners now expect text updates, instant digital invoices, and accurate arrival windows
- Manufacturers increasingly require digital documentation for warranty payouts
- Lost calls and delayed quotes directly reduce conversion rates

### PAIN POINT 3: ENTERPRISE SOFTWARE FOR AN INDUSTRY OF SMALL BUSINESSES
**forLine:** "For appliance repair owners paying enterprise prices for generic tools that don't understand diagnostics, parts, or warranty workflows"

**Bullets:**
1. **ServiceTitan costs $245-$500+ per technician per month** plus $5,000-$50,000 implementation and mandatory 12-month contracts. For a 4-tech appliance repair company, that's $12,000-$24,000/year in software -- a massive overhead for a business doing $400K in revenue.

3. **Workiz is popular among appliance repair companies** but still charges per-user with costs scaling as you add technicians. Features like inventory tracking require higher-tier plans.
4. **None of these platforms are built for appliance repair workflows.** No appliance model/serial number databases, no failure code libraries, no warranty claim documentation workflows, no first-time fix rate analytics. You're paying for a generic scheduling tool.

**Supporting evidence:**
- A 4-tech appliance repair company cannot justify $12K-$24K/year in software costs on $400K revenue
