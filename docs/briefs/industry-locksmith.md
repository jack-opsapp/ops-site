## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- The U.S. locksmith industry is a **$2.9 billion market** with **29,304 businesses**, growing at a CAGR of 4.84% through 2029. The industry has sustained consistent 6.6% annual growth over the past three years, driven by smart lock adoption and mobile service demand.
- Locksmith operations are **emergency-driven and mobile-first**. The United States sees over **16,000 home and car lockouts per day**, requiring 24/7 dispatch capability, rapid response times (30-minute targets), and fully stocked mobile vans. This makes scheduling and dispatch the single most critical operational function.
- Technology adoption is split: larger locksmith companies use general FSM platforms (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber), but the majority of the 29,000+ businesses are **small owner-operator shops** that find these tools overpriced and overbuilt. No major platform offers locksmith-specific features like key code databases, lock hardware inventory tracking, or automotive key programming job workflows.
- Smart lock and electronic access system installation is rapidly changing the service mix, with locksmiths needing to document access credentials, programming codes, and system configurations -- workflows that paper systems and generic FSM tools handle poorly.
- OPS has a clear opportunity: a free-to-start, mobile-first platform with emergency dispatch prioritization, GPS-based technician routing, job documentation with photos, and flat $79/mo pricing that doesn't penalize growth -- directly undercutting ServiceTitan ($245-500/tech/mo) and Housecall Pro ($59-329/mo).

## Industry Profile

### Market Size
- U.S. locksmith industry: **$2.9 billion** (2026)
- **29,304 businesses** in the United States, growing at CAGR of 3.9% (2020-2025)
- Industry revenue has sustained consistent **6.6% annual growth** over the past three years
- Sales projected to grow at a CAGR of **4.84%** between 2025 and 2029
- Highly fragmented market -- no company holds dominant market share
- Over **16,000 home and car lockouts per day** in the United States

### Industry Segments
- **Solo operator / mobile locksmith (majority):** 1-2 people, one van, $80K-$200K revenue
- **Small shop with mobile service:** 2-5 employees, 2-3 vans, $200K-$500K revenue
- **Established operation:** 5-15 employees, 4-8 vans, $500K-$1.5M+ revenue
- **Commercial/institutional specialists:** Access control, master key systems, high-security locks
- **Automotive specialists:** Car key programming, transponder keys, emergency lockouts

### Service Pricing
- Emergency lockout service: **$100-$250/hour**
- Standard lock rekey: $50-$130
- Lock installation: $100-$300 per lock
- Automotive key replacement (transponder): $150-$400
- Smart lock installation: $200-$500
- Commercial master key system: $500-$2,000+
- Average locksmith salary: **$50,490-$55,200/year**
- Entry-level: $34,750 | Journeyman: $50,490 | Master: $60,000-$80,000+
- Owner revenue: **$80,000-$200,000/year** for small companies

### Technology Adoption
- Smart lock and electronic access system adoption is accelerating, changing service mix
- Mobile service delivery is the dominant model -- fully stocked vans operating citywide
- Emergency dispatch requires 24/7 availability and rapid response coordination
- Key code databases and automotive programming tools increasingly digital
- Most small locksmith shops still use paper invoices, phone-based dispatch, and spreadsheets
- Larger operations use general FSM tools but find them lacking locksmith-specific features

### Labor Situation
- Skilled locksmith labor is increasingly difficult to find
- ALOA (Associated Locksmiths of America) certification valued but not mandatory
- Smart lock technology requires ongoing training investment
- Automotive locksmithing increasingly complex with transponder and proximity key systems
- Scam locksmith operations undermine legitimate businesses and customer trust

## Pain Points (3 Blocks)

### PAIN POINT 1: EMERGENCY DISPATCH IS YOUR ENTIRE BUSINESS -- AND YOU'RE RUNNING IT FROM A PHONE
**forLine:** "For locksmith owners juggling 24/7 emergency calls while trying to route technicians efficiently across the city"

**Bullets:**
1. **Over 16,000 lockouts happen every day in the United States.** Your phone rings at 2 AM, and you need to know which tech is closest, which van has the right key blanks, and whether the job is residential, commercial, or automotive -- all before the customer calls someone else.
2. **The first responder wins the job.** In an emergency-driven industry, response time is everything. If your dispatch process involves calling or texting multiple technicians to find who's available, you're losing jobs to competitors with faster systems.
3. **Route inefficiency burns fuel and kills margins.** Mobile locksmiths cover large service areas. Without GPS-based routing and real-time technician location tracking, you're sending crews across town when a closer tech could have handled the call in 15 minutes.
4. **After-hours dispatch is where most small shops break down.** You either take every call yourself or miss revenue. There's no middle ground without a system that can route emergency calls to on-duty technicians automatically.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Emergency locksmiths charge $100-$250/hour, making every missed call significant revenue loss
- 30-minute response time is the competitive standard in major metro areas
- Mobile locksmith operations depend on efficient routing to maximize jobs per shift

### PAIN POINT 2: YOUR VAN IS YOUR SHOP -- AND INVENTORY IS A NIGHTMARE
**forLine:** "For locksmiths managing hundreds of key blanks, lock hardware, and automotive programming tools from a mobile van"

**Bullets:**
1. **A mobile locksmith van carries hundreds of SKUs.** Key blanks (residential, commercial, automotive), lock sets, deadbolts, smart lock kits, transponder programming tools, and specialty hardware. Knowing what's on which van is critical to dispatching the right tech for each job.
2. **Running out of a common key blank on a job means a wasted trip.** The customer waited 30 minutes for you to arrive, and now you can't complete the service. That's a lost job, a frustrated customer, and wasted fuel.
3. **Automotive key programming requires specific tools and inventory.** Transponder keys, proximity fobs, and programming devices vary by vehicle make and model. Sending a tech without the right equipment wastes everyone's time.
4. **No FSM platform tracks locksmith-specific inventory.** General field service tools track "parts" generically. They don't understand key blank catalogs, lock manufacturer SKUs, or automotive key compatibility databases.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Automotive key replacement is a high-margin service ($150-$400) but requires specific inventory per vehicle
- Smart lock installations ($200-$500) require pre-staging specific hardware and programming tools
- Second truck rolls for missing parts directly reduce daily job capacity and profitability

### PAIN POINT 3: SOFTWARE BUILT FOR PLUMBERS, PRICED FOR ENTERPRISES
**forLine:** "For locksmith shop owners paying enterprise prices for generic software that doesn't understand emergency service workflows"

**Bullets:**
1. **ServiceTitan costs $245-$500+ per technician per month** plus $5,000-$50,000 implementation fees and 12+ month contracts. For a 3-tech locksmith operation, that's $9,000-$18,000/year in software alone -- before you've cut a single key.
2. **Housecall Pro scales to $329/month** with add-on packages pushing real costs above $200/month. Per-user pricing punishes growth: adding a van for commercial contracts means adding software costs.
3. **Jobber charges $39-$349/month** with $29/user overages. The Marketing Suite add-on costs an additional $79/month. For a growing locksmith company, costs compound quickly.
4. **None of these platforms understand locksmith workflows.** No emergency priority queuing, no key code databases, no automotive job categorization, no smart lock credential documentation. You're paying enterprise prices for a generic scheduling calendar.

**Supporting evidence:**
- A 3-tech locksmith company making $300K/year cannot justify $10K-$20K in annual software costs
- Per-user pricing penalizes 24/7 operations that need multiple technicians on rotation
- Implementation timelines of 3-6 months (ServiceTitan) are absurd for a business that needs dispatch working today
