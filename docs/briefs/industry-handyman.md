## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- The U.S. handyman services industry is a **$355 billion market** with **550,000 businesses**, making it one of the largest and most fragmented segments of the home services economy. No company holds more than 5% market share. The market is growing rapidly, driven by urbanization, aging housing stock, and homeowners' decreasing willingness to DIY.
- Handyman businesses face a **unique multi-trade challenge**: unlike plumbers or electricians who do one thing, handymen perform dozens of different service types (drywall repair, plumbing fixes, electrical outlets, furniture assembly, painting, carpentry) -- each with different pricing models, skill requirements, and time estimates. Generic FSM tools built for single-trade businesses don't handle this complexity.
- **Pricing and estimating is the #1 operational pain point.** Handymen typically charge $50-$150/hour, but every job is different. Underpricing leads to burnout and financial strain; overpricing loses bids. Unforeseen complications (hidden damage, scope creep) can turn profitable jobs into losses.
- The competitive landscape includes Jobber ($39-349/mo), Housecall Pro ($59-329/mo), ServiceTitan ($245+/tech/mo), and handyman-specific tools like QuoteIQ. Most are either overpriced for solo operators or too generic for multi-trade workflows.
- OPS has a clear opening: a free-to-start, mobile-first platform that handles multi-service scheduling, quick quoting, photo documentation, and customer management at $79/mo flat -- designed for the 550,000 handyman businesses that are too small for ServiceTitan but too busy for spreadsheets.

## Industry Profile

### Market Size
- U.S. handyman services industry: **$355.3 billion** (2025, IBISWorld)
- **550,000 businesses** in the United States, growing at CAGR of 1.9% (2020-2025)
- Global handyman service market: **$4.2 billion** (2024), projected to reach **$12.0 billion** by 2033 at 12.5% CAGR
- Extremely fragmented -- no company holds more than **5% market share**
- Market evolving from fragmented independent workers to a more structured industry with national franchises and on-demand digital platforms

### Industry Segments
- **Solo operator (majority):** 1 person, one truck/van, $40K-$100K revenue
- **Small company:** 2-5 workers, $100K-$350K revenue
- **Established operation:** 5-15 workers, $350K-$1M+ revenue
- **Franchise operations:** Mr. Handyman, Handyman Connection, Ace Handyman Services
- **On-demand platforms:** TaskRabbit, Thumbtack, Angi (lead generation, not full business management)

### Service Pricing
- Average hourly rate: **$50-$150/hour** (national average $60-$85/hour)
- Self-employed handymen: $50-$80/hour
- Average job cost: **$150-$600** depending on scope
- Average homeowner spend: **$408 per project** (range: $65-$1,500)
- Median annual handyman salary: **$48,620/year** ($23.38/hour)
- Most handymen earn: $41,000-$58,000/year

### Common Service Types
- Drywall repair and patching
- Minor plumbing (faucets, toilets, garbage disposals)
- Electrical (outlets, switches, fixtures)
- Carpentry (shelving, trim, door hanging)
- Painting (interior touch-ups, accent walls)
- Furniture assembly (IKEA, office furniture)
- Fixture installation (TVs, blinds, curtain rods)
- General home maintenance (caulking, weather-stripping, door adjustments)
- Pressure washing (decks, driveways, siding)
- Deck and fence repairs

### Technology Adoption
- Most solo handymen still use phone calls, text messages, and paper estimates
- Digital platforms (TaskRabbit, Thumbtack) have introduced transparent pricing expectations
- Customers increasingly expect online booking, text updates, and digital invoicing
- Software adoption is growing but most handymen find enterprise FSM tools too expensive and complex
- On-demand booking platforms are reshaping customer expectations around response time and price transparency

### Labor Situation
- Labor shortages in skilled trades (plumbing, electrical, HVAC) create longer wait times
- Competition from unlicensed service providers drives price pressure
- Multi-trade versatility is the handyman's competitive advantage over specialists
- Younger workers entering through on-demand platforms but often lack business management skills
- Licensing requirements vary dramatically by state and municipality

## Pain Points (3 Blocks)

### PAIN POINT 1: EVERY JOB IS DIFFERENT -- AND PRICING EVERY ONE IS A GAMBLE
**forLine:** "For handyman business owners who've lost money on jobs because hidden damage, scope creep, or underestimating turned a profitable job into a loss"

**Bullets:**
1. **Handymen perform dozens of service types, each with different pricing.** Drywall patch vs. faucet replacement vs. deck repair vs. furniture assembly -- each requires different materials, different time estimates, and different skill levels. No two days look alike, and no single pricing formula works.
2. **Underpricing is the #1 reason handyman businesses fail.** The pressure to compete with unlicensed operators and platform-driven pricing (TaskRabbit, Thumbtack) pushes handymen to undercharge. This is unsustainable -- it leads to burnout, financial strain, and even liability risks.
3. **Unforeseen complications destroy margins.** Hidden termite damage behind a drywall repair, corroded pipes behind a faucet swap, electrical issues behind a light fixture install -- what looked like a 2-hour job becomes a 6-hour job at the same quoted price.
4. **Customers expect transparent pricing before you arrive.** Digital platforms have trained homeowners to expect instant quotes. Without a system to quickly generate professional estimates based on job type, materials, and time, you're either over-quoting (losing the job) or under-quoting (losing money).

**Supporting evidence:**
- Overpricing deters customers, while underpricing leads to burnout and financial strain
- Unforeseen complications (e.g., hidden termite damage in a drywall repair) can turn a profitable job into a loss
- Carpentry, plumbing, and electrical work consistently command higher rates -- but only if priced correctly

### PAIN POINT 2: SCHEDULING CHAOS MULTIPLIES AS YOU GROW
**forLine:** "For handyman businesses drowning in overlapping jobs, lost phone calls, and disorganized customer notes"

**Bullets:**
1. **As the business grows, the weak spots in day-to-day coordination become painfully obvious.** Jobs overlap, updates get lost, and the office spends too much time checking whether work is actually done. What worked for 5 jobs a week breaks at 15.
2. **Missed bookings, late payments, and chaotic schedules frustrate both technicians and clients.** Without centralized scheduling, a new booking gets written on a sticky note, a follow-up gets forgotten, and the customer calls a competitor.
3. **Multi-day and multi-trade jobs create scheduling nightmares.** A kitchen remodel that requires drywall, painting, plumbing, and carpentry across three days -- scheduled around material delivery and customer availability -- is impossible to manage from a phone call log.
4. **Customer communication falls through the cracks.** Confirmation texts, arrival ETAs, follow-up for recurring work -- every missed communication is a missed opportunity for repeat business and referrals.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Lost paperwork, missed phone calls, and disorganized customer notes are the most common operational complaints
- Risk of overbooking and scheduling conflicts increases as job volume grows
- Constant appointment cancellations and rescheduling create headaches for both office and field

### PAIN POINT 3: SOFTWARE BUILT FOR PLUMBERS, PRICED FOR EMPIRES
**forLine:** "For handyman business owners who need more than a spreadsheet but can't justify $300/month for software designed for HVAC companies"

**Bullets:**
1. **ServiceTitan costs $245-$500+ per technician per month** with $5,000-$50,000 implementation fees and 12-month contracts. For a solo handyman making $60K/year, that's absurd. Even for a 5-person company, it's $15,000-$30,000/year in software.
2. **Jobber charges $39-$349/month** with $29/user overages. The Marketing Suite add-on is $79/month extra. Connect plan for teams starts at $169/month. These costs add up fast for a business with tight margins.
3. **Housecall Pro scales to $329/month** with add-on packages (Sales Proposal Tool $40/mo, Flat Rate Price Book $149/mo, GPS Tracking $20/vehicle/mo). Real costs often double the advertised base price.
4. **None of these platforms handle multi-trade workflows naturally.** They're designed for single-trade businesses (plumbing company, HVAC company, electrical company). A handyman who does 15 different types of work needs flexible job categorization, not rigid trade-specific templates.

**Supporting evidence:**
- Handyman businesses have some of the tightest margins in home services due to multi-trade overhead
- Most handyman companies are solo operators or teams of 2-5 -- far too small for enterprise FSM pricing
- QuoteIQ positioned as "#1 rated handyman management software" shows demand for handyman-specific tools
