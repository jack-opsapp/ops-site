## Competitor Profile: Simpro

### Company Facts
- **Founded:** 2002, Brisbane (Eight Mile Plains), Queensland, Australia
- **Co-founders:** Stephen Bradshaw (electrical contractor) and Vaughan McKillop (software engineer); Curtis Thomson also credited
- **Origin story:** Electrical business owner approached a software engineering student about a website; they ended up building job management software in Bradshaw's converted garage
- **CEO:** Gary Specter
- **Total Funding:** ~$397M over 3 rounds from 2 investors
- **Key Investment:** $350M from K1 Investment Management (November 2021), with Level Equity as minority investor
- **Level Equity:** Initial $40M investment in 2016; orchestrated the K1 recapitalization
- **Valuation:** $1B+ (unicorn status, November 2021)
- **Revenue:** $53.9M (reported by Latka); Owler estimates $500M-$1B range [UNVERIFIED -- wide variance suggests different measurement methodologies or inclusion of acquired companies]
- **Employees:** 490-600 (varies by source; Latka says 490, PitchBook says 600, Owler estimates 500-1,000)
- **Customers:** 8,500+ businesses, 250,000+ users worldwide (combined with acquired companies: 17,000+ businesses, 320,000+ users)
- **Offices:** Australia, New Zealand, United Kingdom, United States
- **Key Acquisitions:** ClockShark (US time-sheeting/scheduling) and AroFlo (Australian job management) -- both November 2021; Delight -- January 2026
- **Glassdoor Rating (employer):** 2.8/5 (209 reviews) -- 27% below IT industry average of 3.9; only 40% of employees would recommend

### Pricing (Verified)
- **Published price:** None. Simpro does NOT publish pricing. All quotes require contacting sales.
- **Reported starting point:** ~$30/user/month (from SaaSWorthy and third-party analysis)
- **Basic plan reported at:** US $154/month minimum (from competitor review sites)
- **Typical small business cost (5-10 users):** $300-$600/month, plus several thousand dollars in onboarding
- **Plans:** Simpro Premium, Simpro Premium + Digital Forms, Simpro Premium + Data Feeds -- all custom-priced
- **Onboarding/setup fee:** Mandatory. Covers training, data migration, workflow customization. Cost varies by complexity but described as "thousands of dollars"
- **Implementation timeline:** ~60 days recommended; varies by business complexity
- **Contract terms:** Users report 3-year or 5-year lock-in contracts with 8% per annum increases (CPI + 5%); alternative is threat of 12% annual increases
- **Minimum user requirements:** Simpro reportedly removing flexibility to add/subtract licenses, instead enforcing minimum user quantities
- **Free trial:** NO. Must go through sales process.
- **Add-ons:** Takeoffs (plan measurement/quoting), Digital Forms, Data Feeds, simTRAC (GPS tracking), Simpro Payments (Stripe integration)

### Primary Market
- **Industries:** Electrical (origin trade), plumbing, HVAC, security, fire protection, and broader field services
- **Company sizes:** "Any size" per Simpro -- from crews of 5 to operations of 500. Strongest fit is mid-sized trade and service businesses needing complex job costing and scheduling
- **Geographic focus:** Originally Australia/New Zealand, expanded to UK and US. Still strongest in ANZ markets; US presence growing but competitors like ServiceTitan dominate the US
- **Vertical positioning:** "Total business software for the trades" -- positions as end-to-end from lead to invoice
- **Self-described as:** "AI-first operating platform trusted by 250,000 trades professionals" (as of 2026)

### Known Strengths (Be Honest)
- **End-to-end workflow:** Genuinely comprehensive lead-to-quote-to-job-to-invoice pipeline. Users praise the logical flow from quote to completion
- **Job costing depth:** Detailed cost tracking with actual-vs-estimated comparison, subcontractor costing, and labour allocation. Plumbers and HVAC techs specifically praise this
- **Inventory management:** Vendor catalogue imports, barcode scanning, stock tracking across storage locations, automatic job allocation
- **Reporting and analytics:** Strong reporting capabilities for business decisions -- profitability analysis, performance metrics, job cost reports
- **Accounting integrations:** Xero integration rated 4.3/5; also integrates with QuickBooks, MYOB
- **Scheduling and dispatch:** Visual calendars, drag-and-drop, skill-based matching, multi-day-run planning
- **Asset management:** Maintenance scheduling, compliance tracking, site/asset history
- **Quoting with Takeoffs:** Upload plans, set scale, use smart symbol recognition for faster estimates
- **Mobile invoicing and payments:** Create and send invoices on-site; accept card, check, cash, mobile payments
- **In-person training:** Unlike many competitors, offers in-person training and live US support
- **Mature product:** 24 years in market (founded 2002). Deeply understands trade workflows
- **Global presence:** Supports multi-company, multi-currency operations

### Key Weaknesses
- **ERP-on-phone complexity:** The #1 complaint. The system is so feature-rich that it becomes overwhelming, especially for field staff. "It can feel a bit overwhelming at first because of the many features and functions"
- **Steep learning curve:** Weeks to months of training required. "Learning the software was hard for a lot of our employees." G2 and Capterra reviewers consistently flag this
- **Mobile app quality:** Google Play rating of 3.7/5 with persistent complaints about crashes, data loss, random logouts, and having to reinstall the app monthly. "Never used an app with such a constant stream of issues that just never get fixed"
- **No pricing transparency:** Must go through sales to learn cost. No free trial, no self-serve
- **Contract lock-in:** 3-5 year contracts with 8% annual increases. Users report threats of 12% increases if they don't lock in
- **Expensive onboarding:** "Several thousand dollars" for setup, training, and data migration -- on top of subscription costs
- **Clunky UI:** "Absolute rubbish, long-winded and clunky" (Trustpilot). Interface described as unintuitive, especially for new users
- **Data migration pain:** Onboarding process difficult because previous software often does not allow proper migration. Simpro reportedly does not assist with data migration despite charging for onboarding
- **Customer support response times:** "Support responses take 2-3 days if not weeks" for non-critical issues. Hold times on phone support criticized
- **No GPS tracking for mobile users:** Significant gap for companies needing real-time field technician locations
- **No time tracking verification:** No geofencing or verification for clock in/out, leading to potential time theft
- **Stock control issues:** "Simpro has the worst stock control handling I have ever seen in my 30 plus years in business" (Trustpilot)
- **Internal culture issues:** 2.8/5 Glassdoor rating; reports of toxic leadership, profanity from executives, poor communication about changes
- **Trustpilot rating:** 2.2/5 -- rated "Poor"

## Pain Points (for 3 painPoint blocks)

### Pain Point 1: "An ERP System Disguised as a Mobile App"

**Bullet material:**
- Simpro was built as desktop-first enterprise software over 24 years. The mobile app is an afterthought bolted onto a complex backend. Field crews do not need an ERP -- they need to clock in, see their schedule, and update a job status.
- Google Play rating: 3.7/5 with 710 ratings. Users report "constant stream of issues that just never get fixed."
- Users report the app constantly refreshes, dropdown menus don't work, saved notes are constantly lost, and site history deletes itself.
- "Re-downloading the app every month when it has its big crashes." Multiple users report having to delete and reinstall the app daily to refresh data.
- "The mobile application logs engineers out at random intervals, and there is not a clear reason as to why this happens."
- "Hours logged skipping from am to pm, jumping between days" -- time entry errors directly impact job costing accuracy.
- "No useful features or effort has been put into improving the mobile version for years."
- "Support doesn't do anything to fix bugs or issues."
- No GPS tracking for mobile users. No geofencing for time clock verification. For a field service app, these are foundational gaps.
- The inventory management UI on mobile is described as "a little overwhelming" -- inventory is complex enough on desktop, let alone on a phone with gloves on.

**Target audience line:** Field technicians and crew leaders who spend more time fighting the Simpro app than doing actual work -- deleting, reinstalling, losing notes, getting logged out mid-job.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "I've never used an app with such a constant stream of issues that just never get fixed." -- Google Play review
- "Have to delete the app and re download it due to the app not functioning -- won't refresh any data or upload information/photos or completely wipes any information." -- Google Play review
- "It can feel a bit overwhelming at first because of the many features and functions, especially if you're not on the admin side of the job." -- Capterra review

### Pain Point 2: "60 Days to Set Up. Thousands to Onboard. Years to Lock In."

**Bullet material:**
- Simpro recommends planning for "around 60 days" for implementation. That is two months before your crew can use the software.
- Onboarding fees: "several thousand dollars" for setup, training, and data migration -- and that is BEFORE your first monthly subscription payment.
- Users report Simpro does not actually help with data migration despite charging for onboarding: "the onboarding process was difficult as previous software did not allow for proper migration of essential data."
- Contract lock-in: 3-year or 5-year contracts with 8% per annum increases (CPI + 5%). The alternative is the "threat of a 12% (subject to change) per annum increase."
- Simpro is removing the flexibility to add/subtract user licenses. Minimum user requirements are now enforced, so you pay for seats even if people leave.
- "You have to sign up for a year contract, and even if it does not work for you, you'll still pay for that year." -- Capterra review
- Users report subscription costs doubling over time.
- One user paid "the best part of 1,000 GBP and have now cancelled the package as mis-sold to us." -- Trustpilot
- For comparison, OPS requires no contract, no setup fee, no onboarding cost, and is free to start.

**Target audience line:** Small trade businesses that cannot afford to spend thousands on onboarding, wait 60 days for setup, then get locked into a multi-year contract with automatic price escalations.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "Lock-in contract options of 3-years or 5-years at an 'expected' 8% per annum (CPI + 5%) locked in to a minimum user quantity." -- Capterra review
- "Paid to date the best part of 1,000 GBP and have now cancelled the package as mis-sold to us." -- Trustpilot review
- "The onboarding process was difficult as previous software did not allow for proper migration of essential data." -- Capterra review

### Pain Point 3: "Your Crew Will Never Learn This Software"

**Bullet material:**
- Simpro's aggregate user satisfaction is 82% -- but that 82% is mostly office admins and business owners. The people who hate it are field crews forced to use the mobile app.
- "Learning the software was hard for a lot of our employees." -- Capterra review. This is the fundamental problem: Simpro is built for the office, forced on the field.
- "Training staff to apply business workflows through Simpro has been an ongoing challenge." The learning curve is not a one-time event -- it is perpetual.

- Asset importing is "asset-based and not site-based," requiring "multiple templates when creating a site asset list." This is ERP logic, not field crew logic.

- Compare: OPS is designed so a crew leader can hand their phone to a new worker and that person can be productive in minutes, not weeks.

## FAQ Material (5 questions)

### Q1: "Is Simpro worth it for a small contractor?"
Simpro is designed as comprehensive business management software -- it covers quoting, scheduling, job costing, inventory, invoicing, and asset management. That depth is valuable for mid-sized operations with dedicated admin staff. But for small contractors (under 15 employees), the 60-day implementation, mandatory onboarding fees of several thousand dollars, and 3-5 year contract lock-ins make it a risky investment. Users report that "learning the software was hard for a lot of our employees" and that training is an "ongoing challenge." If you are a small crew that needs to schedule jobs, track time, and invoice clients, simpler platforms deliver what you need without the ERP overhead. OPS is free to start and built specifically for field crews.

### Q2: "Does Simpro have a free trial?"
No. Simpro does not offer a free trial or a free plan. You must contact their sales team and go through an implementation process before accessing the software. By contrast, OPS is free to download with no credit card, no sales call, and no demo required. You can evaluate OPS with your actual crew on actual job sites before committing anything.

### Q3: "How much does Simpro actually cost?"
Simpro does not publish pricing. All quotes require contacting sales. Third-party sources report pricing starting around $30/user/month, with a base plan starting at approximately $154/month. For a 5-10 person crew, expect $300-$600/month in subscription costs PLUS several thousand dollars in mandatory onboarding fees. Add 3-5 year contracts with 8% annual price increases, and the total cost of ownership over 3 years is substantial. Users report subscription costs doubling over time.

### Q4: "Why is Simpro so complicated?"
Simpro started in 2002 as desktop business management software and has spent 24 years adding features -- job costing, inventory management, asset tracking, project management, quoting, invoicing, compliance, and more. The result is a system that can do almost anything, but requires significant training to operate. Users describe the interface as "clunky and unintuitive" and the feature set as "overwhelming." The mobile app compounds this problem by trying to squeeze desktop-grade complexity onto a phone screen. If your field crew just needs to see their schedule, clock in, update job status, and take photos, they do not need an ERP system in their pocket.

### Q5: "What are the main problems with Simpro's mobile app?"
The Simpro Mobile app (3.7/5 on Google Play, 710 ratings) has persistent issues: random logouts mid-job, data that does not sync or refresh, notes that disappear, dropdown menus that fail, and crashes requiring complete app reinstallation -- sometimes daily. Users report "never used an app with such a constant stream of issues that just never get fixed." The app also lacks GPS tracking for field technicians and has no geofencing for time clock verification, which are basic requirements for field service management.
