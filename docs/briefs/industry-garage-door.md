## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- **The US garage door service market is valued at $4.78 billion (2025) and growing at 5.9% CAGR**, with the broader garage and overhead door market at $9.94 billion. Large, fragmented market with strong tailwinds from aging housing stock, smart home adoption, and labor shortages.
- **The industry is overwhelmingly small business**: most garage door companies operate with 2-10 technicians. DASMA estimates 3,000+ independent dealers nationwide.
- **Emergency/same-day service is the defining operational challenge**: 78% of incoming calls are classified as "urgent" by the customer, though only 23% require true emergency dispatch. Mishandling this dynamic costs companies $1,600-$3,200/month in lost revenue.
- **Software incumbents are a poor fit**: ServiceTitan costs $250-500/tech/month with 3-12 month onboarding and lacks garage door manufacturer catalog integrations. Workiz ($275/mo for 5 users) is plagued by app crashes, dropped calls, and unreliable support.
- **OPS has a clear opening**: flat $79/mo pricing, crew-first mobile UX, offline mode, Spanish language support, and zero implementation timeline directly address the top pain points.

## 3. PAIN POINTS

### PAIN POINT 1: Emergency Dispatch Chaos

Garage door repair is one of the most emergency-driven trades. A broken spring or stuck door means a family can't leave their home or secure their property.

**Proof Point 1 — Misclassification wastes resources:**
78% of garage door service calls are classified as urgent by customers but only 23% require true emergency response.

**Proof Point 2 — Missed calls are lost revenue:**
A typical garage door repair business receives 50 calls monthly, with 85% not leaving voicemails — approximately 42 potential customers missed, representing **$6,300 monthly in potential revenue loss**.

**Proof Point 3 — Emergency calls disrupt entire schedules:**
"Emergency garage door calls can derail your entire day's operations if you don't have the right process in place. Companies must reserve 15-20% of daily schedule as flexible capacity."

**Proof Point 4 — Revenue at stake is massive:**
The average small contracting business loses **$45,000-$120,000 per year** to unanswered phone calls.

### PAIN POINT 2: Parts Inventory & Truck Stock Complexity

Garage door work requires technicians to carry a wide variety of specialized parts — torsion springs of various wire gauges and lengths, extension springs, cables, rollers, hinges, weather seals, opener components. Running out of a common spring means a second trip and lost afternoon.

**Proof Point 1 — The SKU problem:**
A single truck may need **40-60+ spring SKUs** to handle common residential doors (multiple wire diameters, inside diameters, and lengths).

**Proof Point 2 — Supply chain pressure:**
"Fluctuations in material availability often create uncertainty and make it difficult for suppliers to maintain production schedules and meet customer demand."

**Proof Point 3 — Cash flow impact:**
"Effective inventory management keeps your team moving and your customers happy, ensuring the right products are in the right place at the right time without tying up unnecessary cash."

**Proof Point 4 — Return trips kill margins:**
When a tech shows up without the right torsion spring, that's a return trip, a rescheduled customer, and a net loss. Most FSM tools lack industry-specific parts catalogs.

### PAIN POINT 3: Software Not Built for Garage Door Companies

The FSM market is dominated by HVAC/plumbing platforms that bolt on "garage door" as a marketing label without adapting the product.

**Proof Point 1 — ServiceTitan's catalog gap:**
> "ServiceTitan is pushing for Garage Door Companies to sign up with them, but they are not integrated with any of the manufacturers catalogs, yet they do this for HVAC." — Trustpilot review

**Proof Point 2 — Enterprise tools overwhelm small shops:**
> "ServiceTitan is designed for larger operations (20+ technicians). Small businesses often find ServiceTitan too expensive, too complex, and overwhelming."

**Proof Point 3 — Onboarding failures:**
> "The reason they give you 3 months free in the beginning is because it will take minimum 3 months to figure out how to use it because the onboarding is so bad." — Capterra review

**Proof Point 4 — Generic software creates complexity:**
"A garage door company software that's hard to use costs you time and money."

## 5. OPS DIFFERENTIATORS FOR GARAGE DOOR

### $79/mo Flat Pricing
A 5-tech garage door company saves **$14,000-$29,000/year** switching from ServiceTitan to OPS. For a business with 10-15% net margins doing $1M/year ($120K profit), ServiceTitan takes 12-25% of total profit. OPS takes less than 1%.

### Offline Mode in Garages
Garage door technicians work inside garages — concrete structures notorious for cell signal dead zones. Plus suburban and rural areas with spotty coverage.

### Spanish Language Support
Hispanics comprise 31% of the US construction workforce. In Sun Belt states where garage door demand is highest, Spanish-speaking technicians are often the majority of field crews.

### Key Features Garage Door Companies Need
1. Fast emergency dispatch — reroute a tech in seconds
2. Truck stock tracking — know which tech has which springs
3. Variable job pricing — not flat-rate; jobs vary by door type, spring system, opener brand
4. Photo documentation — before/after for warranty and liability
5. Quick on-site invoicing — get paid before leaving the driveway
6. Customer communication — automated "tech is on the way" texts
