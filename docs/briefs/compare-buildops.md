## Competitor Profile: BuildOps

### Company Facts
- **Founded:** August 2018, Santa Monica, CA
- **Total Funding:** $273M (Series C at $127M in March 2025, led by Meritech Capital)
- **Valuation:** $1B (unicorn status, March 2025)
- **Revenue:** $97.4M (as of mid-2025); revenue doubled in both 2023 and 2024
- **Employees:** ~575 (45% growth year-over-year)
- **Customers:** 1,000+ commercial contractors
- **Average Revenue Per Customer:** ~$97K/year (derived from $97.4M / 1,000 customers)
- **CEO:** Alok Chanani
- **Key Investors:** Meritech Capital, Founders Fund, Bond Capital, Next47, Fika Ventures, SE Ventures

### Pricing (Verified)
- **Published price:** None. BuildOps does not publish pricing. All quotes require a sales call and demo.
- **Reported starting point:** ~$299/user/month billed annually (from ITQlick and multiple third-party sources)
- **Lower-end estimates:** Some sources report $49-$150/user/month for basic tiers; the variance suggests heavy custom pricing based on company size and feature needs.
- **For a 10-person crew at $299/user/month:** $2,990/month = **$35,880/year**
- **For a 20-person operation:** $71,760/year
- **Implementation fees:** $1,000-$5,000 for small businesses; tens of thousands for enterprise
- **No free trial.** No free plan. Must book a demo with sales to see the product.
- **Contract structure:** Annual billing, custom terms. No month-to-month option evident.
- **ITQlick pricing score:** 4 out of 10

### Primary Market
- Commercial contractors ONLY (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, fire/life safety, refrigeration)
- Does NOT serve residential contractors at all
- Best fit: medium to large commercial service companies (50-500+ employees)
- Their own materials say residential contractors should look elsewhere

### Known Strengths (Be Honest)
- Purpose-built for commercial contractor workflows (project management, service agreements, multi-phase jobs)
- Strong calendar management (scored 10.0 on G2)
- Reporting capabilities rated highly (9.2 on G2)
- Customer support rated 9.8 on G2 (when you can reach them)
- Real-time field-to-office data flow
- Photo, video, audio capture on work orders
- Offline mobile capability
- QuickBooks integration (when it works)
- $1B valuation means continued product investment

### Key Weaknesses
- **Price shock:** $299/user/month is 5-10x what most small contractors budget for FSM
- **No pricing transparency:** Must go through sales gauntlet to learn cost
- **No free trial:** 46% of FSM competitors offer free trials; BuildOps does not
- **Ease of setup score: 6.7/10 on G2** (vs. Jobber at 8.8)
- **Implementation timeline:** Target is 8 weeks; user reports of months or even a year
- **Commercial-only:** Zero residential support; mixed-trade crews are out of luck
- **Overkill for small teams:** Features designed for 50+ person operations
- **QuickBooks sync bugs:** Duplicate exports, sync failures reported repeatedly
- **Steep learning curve:** Weeks-to-months of training sessions
- **Mobile app limitations:** Desktop-first; mobile doesn't have feature parity
- **Not yet profitable** despite $97M revenue (aggressive scaling, high burn)

### Review Scores
- **Capterra:** 4.5/5 (149 reviews) -- 95% positive, 3% negative
- **G2:** ~47 reviews, strong in some categories but ease of setup drags
- **User Satisfaction (aggregate):** 91% across platforms
- **Glassdoor (employer):** 3.1/5 work-life balance; hire-and-fire culture reported

## Pain Points (for 3 painPoint blocks)

### Pain Point 1: "$299 Per User Per Month. Per. User."

**Bullet material:**
- BuildOps charges approximately $299/user/month billed annually. For a 10-person crew, that is $35,880/year. For 20 people, $71,760/year. Before implementation fees.
- BuildOps does not publish pricing anywhere. You must book a sales call, sit through a demo, and get a "custom quote" -- which multiple sources confirm lands around $299/user.
- Implementation fees add $1,000-$5,000 for small businesses, potentially tens of thousands for larger operations.
- The average revenue per BuildOps customer is ~$97,000/year (derived from their $97.4M revenue across ~1,000 customers). That is what they extract from each account.
- ITQlick gave BuildOps a pricing score of 4 out of 10.
- No free trial. No free plan. No way to test before committing. 46% of FSM competitors offer free trials.
- One reviewer noted: "The cost of BuildOps was significantly higher than their previous field management software provider."
- For context, Jobber starts at $39/month for 1 user. A 10-person team on Jobber costs roughly $4,188-$8,988/year depending on plan. BuildOps costs 4-8x more.

**Target audience line:** Small and mid-size commercial contractors (5-30 employees) who got a BuildOps quote and cannot justify $36K-$72K/year for scheduling software.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "There really isn't much positive I can say about BuildOps. Low entry costs, for the program, but high end user support required." (Capterra)
- "The sales team makes promises the program has yet to deliver, even after lengthy rollout periods." (Review aggregator)
- "Businesses with smaller budgets or those needing immediate pricing transparency may find BuildOps alternatives more suitable." (Research.com)

### Pain Point 2: "Implementation That Takes Months, Not Minutes"

**Bullet material:**
- BuildOps targets an 8-week implementation timeline. User reality: "Been trying to implement for a year and still not working!" (Capterra, 1-star review)
- G2 ease-of-setup score: 6.7 out of 10. Compare to Jobber at 8.8. BuildOps is the hardest to set up among major FSM platforms.
- "Many users reported their transition to BuildOps was anything but smooth, often requiring weeks (or even months) of training sessions." (Multiple review platforms)
- Some businesses had to adjust their internal workflows to fit BuildOps rather than the other way around.
- "The lack of implementation support, poor processes, and abysmal communication have made this one of the worst vendor experiences we have ever had." (Review site)
- "Poor experience, we gave up ClickUp for this ineffective program which requires constant verification of the data it exported." (Capterra)
- Users struggle with platform terminology, requiring frequent assistance from support.
- Post-go-live: daily check-ins for a couple weeks, then twice weekly. This is not software you install and use -- it is a project.
- QuickBooks sync issues compound the pain: items export multiple times, sync thinks it failed when it succeeded, creating duplicate records in accounting.

**Target audience line:** Contractors who need software working this week, not after 8 weeks of training sessions and data migration.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "Horrible experience!! Been trying to implement for a year and still not working!" (Capterra)
- "The initial setup process [is] clunky, with some features needing workarounds to function properly." (GetApp)
- "BuildOps can have a significant learning curve, lengthening training and implementation time." (Research.com)

### Pain Point 3: "Built for Enterprise. You Are Not Enterprise."

**Bullet material:**
- BuildOps is designed for medium-to-large commercial contractors managing multi-phase projects, service agreements, and large field teams. If you are a 5-15 person crew, you are paying for a platform built for companies 5-10x your size.
- Commercial ONLY. BuildOps explicitly does not serve residential contractors. If your crew does both residential and commercial work, BuildOps cannot support half your business.
- "For very small contractors focused solely on residential service work, some of the commercial features may feel like overkill." (Review ecosystem)
- "Although usable by smaller businesses, BuildOps is primarily tailored for medium to large contractors due to its extensive features and pricing structure." (Research.com)
- The platform has features like submittal management, multi-phase project tracking, and service agreement automation -- powerful for a 200-person MEP contractor, unnecessary overhead for a crew of 8.
- "If your company values industry-specific tools and can invest in onboarding and customization, BuildOps may be a worthwhile investment." Translation: if you have the budget AND the bandwidth for months of setup.
- BuildOps' average customer pays ~$97K/year. That is not small-business money.

**Target audience line:** Small commercial contractors and mixed residential/commercial crews who were told BuildOps was "the answer" but found it was built for companies 10x their size.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "BuildOps is the answer when you need a tool that supports the challenges of large-scale commercial projects." (Workyard review)
- "Users find setup difficulties with BuildOps, noting it requires time and effort before realizing its full potential, and the complex setup can be challenging and time-consuming for smaller organizations." (GetApp)
- Reviewer rated overall experience "about a 2.5 out of 10." (ITQlick)

## FAQ Material (5 questions)

### Q1: "Is BuildOps worth it for a small contractor?"
BuildOps is designed for medium-to-large commercial contractors. Their average customer pays approximately $97,000 per year. At $299/user/month, a 10-person crew would spend $35,880/year before implementation fees. For small contractors (under 20 employees), simpler and more affordable platforms deliver the scheduling, dispatching, and job tracking you actually need without the enterprise overhead. OPS is free to start and built specifically for field crews.

### Q2: "Does BuildOps have a free trial?"
No. BuildOps does not offer a free trial or a free plan. You must contact their sales team, book a demo, and go through an onboarding process before accessing the software. By contrast, 46% of FSM platforms offer free trials. OPS is free to download with no credit card, no sales call, and no demo required.

### Q3: "How long does BuildOps implementation take?"
BuildOps targets 8 weeks for implementation. However, user reviews report implementation timelines ranging from 3 months to over a year, with one Capterra reviewer stating "Been trying to implement for a year and still not working." The platform has a G2 ease-of-setup score of 6.7/10, the lowest among major FSM competitors.

### Q4: "Can I use BuildOps for residential work?"
No. BuildOps is built exclusively for commercial contractors. It does not support residential service workflows. If your company handles any residential jobs, or does both commercial and residential work, you will need a separate system for that half of your business. OPS supports both commercial and residential work in a single platform.

### Q5: "What does BuildOps actually cost?"
BuildOps does not publish pricing. All quotes require a sales call and demo. Third-party sources consistently report pricing around $299/user/month billed annually. For a 10-person team, that works out to approximately $35,880/year, plus implementation fees of $1,000-$5,000+. BuildOps received a pricing score of 4/10 from ITQlick.
