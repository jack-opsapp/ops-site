## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- The US pool cleaning services market is worth **$8.8 billion** (2025), serving **10.7 million pools** across **78,817 businesses**, growing at a CAGR of 4.2%. The market is radically fragmented — no company holds more than 5% share.
- Pool service is a **route-density business**, not a project business. Technicians service 8-12 pools per day across fixed weekly routes. Inefficient routing wastes 45+ minutes per tech per day — nearly a full workday per week per technician lost to windshield time.
- The dominant pool-specific software is **Skimmer** (35,000+ users), which angered its user base with a **100% price increase** ($1 to $2 per pool/month) in 2024, creating an active switching window. **Pool Brain** targets larger operations (35+ trucks) but has battery drain and Android stability complaints.
- OPS has a strong opening: flat pricing ($79/mo) directly undercuts Skimmer's per-pool model that punishes growth. Crew-first mobile UX, offline mode, and Spanish-language support address real unmet needs.
- **62% of pool service companies operate with 0-1 office employees** and **47% have 2-6 crew members** — exactly the profile OPS is built for.

## 3. PAIN POINTS

### PAIN POINT 1: Route Management Chaos — Windshield Time Kills Profitability

Pool service is fundamentally a route-density business. Every minute driving is a minute not servicing pools. Techs hit 8-12+ stops per day on fixed weekly routes. Inefficient routing compounds daily.

**Proof Point 1 — 45+ minutes lost per tech per day** to inefficient routing — 7.5 hours weekly per technician, nearly a full workday lost to windshield time.

**Proof Point 2 — Poorly routed techs service 6-7 pools instead of 8-10** — a 20-30% productivity loss. For a 10-tech company at $200/stop, poor routing costs **$8,000-$12,000 monthly**.

**Proof Point 3 — Inefficient routes waste 30-40% more fuel** as technicians zigzag across town instead of servicing clustered neighborhoods.

**Proof Point 4 — Fragmented tool stacks:** Pool service owners report juggling multiple apps — one described ending up with "Skimmer, Quickbooks, and Gusto" just to manage a 4-route operation, piecing together scheduling, billing, and team tracking across three separate platforms.

### PAIN POINT 2: Getting Paid — Recurring Billing Is a Manual Nightmare

Pool service runs on recurring monthly contracts ($140-$160/pool/month typical). Billing should be automatic, but for most operators it is not.

**Proof Point 1 — Pool companies lose approximately $3,000 per year** to customers who do not pay, with some carrying 12+ delinquent accounts at any given time.

**Proof Point 2 — Reducing average collection time from 18 days to 5 days** can improve cash flow by **$35,000-$50,000** in reduced outstanding receivables for mid-size operations.

**Proof Point 3 — Skimmer billing limitations:**
> "Minor issues with the billing, including the inability to invoice right from a work order and to have the system know if the client has auto-pay available." — Capterra review

**Proof Point 4 — Elderly clients resist email invoicing**, commercial customers demand PDF invoices which Skimmer reportedly cannot produce. Managing mixed billing methods scales poorly.

### PAIN POINT 3: Software That Punishes Growth — Per-Pool Pricing Models

The dominant pool-specific software (Skimmer) charges per pool served. Every new customer raises your software cost.

**Proof Point 1 — Skimmer's 100% price increase** from $1 to $2/pool/month in 2024 generated immediate backlash:
> "Guess what Skimmer, NO ITS NOT! I have just signed with poolservice.software."

**Proof Point 2 — At 200 pools, Skimmer costs $400/month.** At 300 pools, $600/month. At 500 pools, $1,000/month. Every new customer increases operating cost.

**Proof Point 3 — Pool Brain charges $55/month per active field technician** plus $10/month for office users. A 10-tech operation pays $560/month — before addressing their reported battery drain that kills phones mid-route.

**Proof Point 4 — Operators rank pricing predictability as a top factor** when choosing software — yet market leaders all use variable pricing that changes as you grow.

## 4. COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### COMPETITOR 1: Skimmer

**Overview:** Dominant pool-specific software. 35,000+ users servicing 1M+ pools monthly.

**Pricing:**
- $98/month base + $2/month per additional serviced location
- Previously $1/pool; doubled to $2 in 2024
- 100 pools = $298/mo | 200 pools = $498/mo | 500 pools = $1,098/mo

**What Pool Pros Love:**
- Purpose-built for pool service
- LSI water chemistry calculator powered by Orenda
- Route optimization cuts drive time by up to 25%
- Offline mobile functionality
- Automated service reports with photos

**What Pool Pros Hate:**
- **Per-pool pricing that punishes growth** — #1 complaint post-2024
- Billing system has "kinks" — can't invoice from work orders, no PDF invoices for commercial
- App is **slow to update** and cannot run on multiple devices
- Keeps adding features that "complicate and crowd what was previously a simpler app"
- Cannot upload videos during service stops
- Charges per text message sent to clients

### COMPETITOR 2: Pool Brain

**Overview:** Targets medium-to-large operations (35+ trucks). Analytics and operations platform.

**Pricing:**
- $10/month for up to 50 office users
- $55/month per active field technician
- 5 techs = $285/mo | 10 techs = $560/mo | 20 techs = $1,110/mo

**What Pool Pros Love:**
- Robust analytics, reports, and alerts
- Drag-and-drop technician management
- Real-time map view of job statuses
- Forces proof of work (photo evidence)
- "Replaced the workload of 3 or 4 people"

**What Pool Pros Hate:**
- **Battery drain is severe** — "after one pool you're at 70%"
- Android app stability issues
- "Unresponsive features" and runs slow
- Smaller community (27 Capterra reviews vs. Skimmer's 213)
- Built for enterprise complexity, not small teams

### Other Competitors

### Crew-First Matters for Pool Service
In 62% of companies there is zero or one office employee — the tech IS the scheduler, service provider, customer communicator, and invoicer. 56dp touch targets matter when reaching for a phone with wet hands. Dark theme matters in Arizona sunlight.

### Offline Mode
Pool techs service properties from gated communities with basements to rural ranch properties miles from cell towers. Pool Brain's offline mode is undermined by battery drain. Skimmer offers offline but the app is slow. OPS's dark theme and lightweight architecture means full route days without connectivity anxiety.

### Spanish-Language Support
Hispanics projected to account for **78% of net new US workers** between 2020-2030. Pool service heavily employs immigrant labor markets. **No major pool-specific software offers native Spanish-language support.** A tech who can't read route instructions in their primary language will resist using the app.

### Key Features Pool Service Companies Need
1. Route optimization and daily route view (the core workflow)
2. Service completion tracking with photos (proof of work)
3. Automated recurring billing (85%+ of revenue is recurring)
4. Chemical reading logging (compliance and customer trust)
5. Customer communication (automated notifications)
6. Work order management (one-off repairs alongside routes)
7. Offline capability (reliability at remote properties)
8. Simple, fast mobile UX (techs use this 8-12 times per day)
