## Competitor Profile: FieldEdge

### Company Facts
- **Founded:** 1980 as Coastal Computer Corporation (Fort Myers, FL) by George and Glenn Slay, HVAC contractors who built the first electronic dispatch board
- **Original product name:** ESC (Electronic Service Control), created under dESCO, LLC after Dean Schreiner acquired Coastal Computer Corporation from the Slay brothers
- **Rebranded:** "FieldEdge" is the current brand name for what was previously dESCO/ESC
- **Acquired:** March 2018 by Advent International (private equity); merged with Clearent (payment processing) to create what became Xplor Technologies
- **Parent Company:** Xplor Technologies (Advent International portfolio company). Xplor serves 106,000+ businesses across childcare, fitness, field services, and personal services; processes $37B+ in payments annually. Advent explored $4B IPO valuation for Xplor in 2021; currently pursuing a $3-4B continuation vehicle (March 2025).
- **Headquarters:** 1525 Hendry St, Fort Myers, Florida 33901 (also offices in Atlanta)
- **CEO:** Jay Rock [UNVERIFIED -- leadership has changed multiple times; Connie Certusi was appointed President in 2018; Steve Lau and Rameez Ansari were Co-CEOs prior]
- **Employees:** ~201-300 (sources vary; RocketReach reports 201 across 4 continents as of Jan 2025; ZoomInfo reports 300)
- **Customers:** ~40,000 users total (Latka, 2021 data). 6sense reports ~196 new company adoptions in 2025.
- **Revenue:** $21.7M in 2021 (Latka); estimates of $56.5M-$75M in 2025 [UNVERIFIED -- sources conflict]
- **Market Share:** 0.86% of FSM market (6sense). Top competitors by market share: UpKeep (35.7%), ServiceTitan (12.5%), Intuit FSM (7.6%)
- **Key Investors:** Advent International (majority stake), Highland Creek Partners, Hunter Search Capital, Pacific Lake, Relay Investments, Search Fund Partners
- **Notable acquisitions by Xplor/FieldEdge:** Coolfront Technologies (flat-rate pricing app, merged with FieldEdge); Enterprise Selling Solutions / HVACBizPro (August 2022, added Proposal Pro); Wintac (FSM solution)

### Pricing (Verified from Multiple Sources)

- **Published price:** Partially. FieldEdge's pricing page shows three tiers (Select, Premier, Elite) but requires a demo call for exact quotes.
- **Per-user cost:** $100/user/month for office users (dispatchers, management); $125/user/month for field technicians
- **Setup fee:** $500-$2,000 one-time (higher end includes implementation training)
- **Onboarding:** Mandatory 5-week onboarding process for all new customers (data migration, training, configuration)
- **For a 5-person crew (3 techs + 2 office):** $575/month = **$6,900/year** + setup fees
- **For a 10-person crew (7 techs + 3 office):** $1,175/month = **$14,100/year** + setup fees
- **For a 20-person operation (14 techs + 6 office):** $2,350/month = **$28,200/year** + setup fees
- **Payment processing:** FieldEdge Payments powered by Clearent (Xplor-owned). Users report 2.7%-3.4% per transaction. Multiple users report being promised 2.7% but charged 3.4%.
- **No free trial.** FieldEdge does not offer a free trial because "they find new members have the most success when their team walks them through the onboarding process." Trial demos reportedly only available to "elite" customers.
- **Contract structure:** Advertised as month-to-month. However, annual contracts are also offered. Annual plans do not issue refunds if you cancel early, and users report being stuck in automatic annual renewals they were unaware of.

### Plan Tiers

**Select (Entry-Level)**
- Dispatching, booking & scheduling
- Basic agreements & quotes
- Customer management
- Pricebook & flat rate pricing
- Support: chat only

**Premier (Mid-Level)**
- Everything in Select

**Elite (Premium)**

- Support: dedicated customer success representative

### Primary Market
- **Core trades:** HVAC, plumbing, electrical (residential service focus)
- **Also serves:** Appliance repair, locksmith, and general home service contractors
- **Company size:** Small to mid-size businesses (majority of customers in 20-49 employee range per 6sense)
- **Geographic focus:** United States (68.3% of customers), United Kingdom (8.2%), Canada (6.6%)
- **Sweet spot:** Service-oriented contractors who run flat-rate pricing books and rely on QuickBooks for accounting
- **Weak fit:** Installation-heavy contractors, commercial-only operations, or businesses that need modern estimating tools

### Known Strengths (Be Honest)
- **QuickBooks integration is best-in-class:** FieldEdge invented the first full QuickBooks FSM integration in 1999. Two-way sync handles customers, items, invoices, and payments. QuickBooks Platinum Partner. Users report saving 20+ hours/week on double-entry.
- **Flat-rate pricing books:** Deep pricebook management with Coolfront integration. If your business is 80% service calls with flat-rate pricing, this is a genuine strength.
- **Service agreement management:** Automates recurring maintenance schedules, billing, and customer notifications. Reviewers specifically praise this feature.
- **Established/proven platform:** 40+ years in the industry means mature, battle-tested workflows for traditional HVAC/plumbing/electrical service operations.
- **Dispatching and scheduling:** Real-time dispatch board is functional and well-understood by long-time users.
- **No long-term contract required:** Month-to-month billing is available (though annual plans exist and carry cancellation risks).
- **Customer management:** Comprehensive customer history, service records, and equipment tracking.

### Key Weaknesses
- **Outdated, legacy interface:** Built in the desktop software era and "progressively modernized -- sometimes awkwardly." Interface relies heavily on text entry boxes, dialog buttons, and pop-up forms. Multiple reviewers describe it as "dated," "clunky," and "old."
- **Abysmal mobile app ratings:** 1.8/5 on Apple App Store (~370 ratings); 2.0/5 on Google Play Store (~200 reviews). Constant crashes, lost quotes, sync failures, keyboard freezes.
- **High cost for what you get:** $100-$125/user/month plus $500-$2,000 setup, plus mandatory 5-week onboarding. Features score 3.9/5 and Value for Money 3.9/5 on Capterra -- below average.
- **Payment processing lock-in:** Owned by Xplor/Clearent, users report being locked into Clearent for payment processing with no alternative. Bait-and-switch on processing rates (promised 2.7%, charged 3.4%).
- **Support quality is inconsistent:** While some praise support, others report "support representatives won't even email or call back" and "Leave a message and we'll return your call NEVER." Select plan users only get chat support.
- **Weak for installation/estimating work:** Designed around service calls and flat-rate pricing. Estimating features "feel like an afterthought" for installation-focused contractors.
- **Updates cause disruption:** Users report updates introduce new bugs, change workflows without warning, and the company "makes a lot of useless changes and ignores the changes that would benefit everyone."
- **No free trial:** Cannot test before committing. Must go through sales demo process.
- **Missing modern features:** No native text notifications for customers, no native review management tools (requires Podium at $249+/month), limited automation compared to competitors.
- **Data migration nightmares:** Users report QuickBooks data transfers failing (item costs not transferring for 2,400+ items), requiring manual re-entry.
- **Onboarding complaints:** Multiple users report rushed, unhelpful onboarding with 2-3 day response times from onboarding agents, unanswered questions, and "nothing smooth about the overall implementation process."

## Pain Points (for 3 painPoint blocks)

### Pain Point 1: "Built in the '80s. Feels Like It."

**Bullet material:**
- FieldEdge traces its roots to 1980 when the Slay brothers created the first electronic dispatch board. 45+ years later, that legacy shows. The interface "relies heavily on text entry boxes, dialog buttons, and pop-up forms" and "can feel dated, impacting user experience."
- FieldEdge was built in an era of desktop software and has been "progressively modernized -- sometimes awkwardly." Modern software offers mobile-first apps that techs enjoy using. FieldEdge offers a mobile app with 1.8 stars.
- Users report "there are so many steps for even the simplest of tasks, and it should not take 10-20 minutes to create one work order."
- The mobile app constantly crashes, loses data, and fails to sync. One user: "Once you learn how to navigate past the bugs and glitches, it works fine a third of the time." A third of the time.
- Employees using the app have said they will quit if it is not replaced. That is not a software problem -- it is a retention problem.
- When FieldEdge pushes updates, they often introduce new bugs. "The constant glitches which leads to multiple freezes in a day is asinine and ridiculous. This causes files and pictures to be lost even when you believe it was saved."
- The software seems "a bit dated; some features and functions that many new estimating/scheduling software packages are implementing are not included."

**Target audience line:** HVAC, plumbing, and electrical contractors whose techs dread using FieldEdge's mobile app and whose office staff wastes time fighting a legacy interface.

## FAQ Material (5 questions)

### Q1: "Is FieldEdge worth it for a small contractor?"
FieldEdge charges $100-$125/user/month plus $500-$2,000 in setup fees and requires a 5-week onboarding process. For a 10-person crew, that is approximately $14,100/year before add-ons. The platform was originally designed for desktop use in the 1980s and has been incrementally modernized. The mobile app has a 1.8/5 rating on the App Store. For small contractors who need software that works on day one without weeks of training, simpler and more affordable platforms exist. OPS is free to start and designed mobile-first for field crews.

### Q2: "Does FieldEdge have a free trial?"
No. FieldEdge does not offer a free trial. They state this is because "new members have the most success when their team walks them through the onboarding process." You must request a demo, go through a sales call, and commit to a paid plan with a setup fee before using the software. By contrast, OPS is free to download with no credit card, no sales call, and no mandatory onboarding.

### Q3: "Why is FieldEdge's mobile app rated so low?"
FieldEdge's mobile app has a 1.8/5 rating on the Apple App Store and 2.0/5 on Google Play. Users consistently report crashes (at least once daily), lost quotes and data when saving, keyboard freezing that requires app restarts, GPS failures, and poor offline functionality. The core product was designed as desktop software in the 1980s and the mobile app often feels like an afterthought. FieldEdge has released multiple separate app versions (FE 2.0 Beta, FieldEdge 3) rather than fixing the original.

### Q4: "How does FieldEdge compare to ServiceTitan?"
FieldEdge and ServiceTitan both target HVAC, plumbing, and electrical contractors, but differ significantly. ServiceTitan is more expensive ($245-$399/tech/month) but offers more automation features and a more modern interface. FieldEdge is cheaper per user but has a legacy interface and much lower mobile app ratings. Both require sales demos and neither offers free trials. For contractors who want modern software without enterprise pricing, OPS offers a free tier with a mobile-first design.

### Q5: "What does FieldEdge actually cost in total?"
FieldEdge charges $100/month per office user and $125/month per field technician, plus a one-time setup fee of $500-$2,000. There is a mandatory 5-week onboarding process. Payment processing through Clearent (required) adds 2.7%-3.4% per credit card transaction. Advanced features like review management require third-party tools like Podium ($249+/month extra). Annual plans do not refund if you cancel early. Total cost for a 10-person team: approximately $14,100-$17,000+/year depending on plan tier and add-ons.
