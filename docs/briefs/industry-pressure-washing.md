## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- The U.S. pressure washing services industry generates **$1.2 billion in revenue** across **32,193 businesses** employing approximately 41,799 technicians. The industry has grown by an average of **5.7% per year** from 2018-2024, driven by increasing property maintenance standards and commercial contract demand.
- Pressure washing is a **route-density and seasonal business**. Operators service multiple properties per day along optimized routes, with **peak season running March through October** in most climates. Southern states stay busy year-round, but northern operators face 3-4 months of near-zero revenue during winter.
- **Speed to respond is everything**: 78% of customers buy from the first company to respond, yet the average business response time is 47 hours and 71% of leads never receive any response at all. This represents millions in lost revenue across the industry.
- The competitive landscape includes Jobber ($39-349/mo), Housecall Pro ($59-329/mo), ServiceMonster (purpose-built for exterior cleaning), and CrewNest ($49+/mo for pressure washers). No platform dominates the segment, and pricing models that scale per-user penalize the seasonal hiring that pressure washing companies depend on.
- OPS has a clear opening: flat $79/mo pricing that doesn't punish seasonal crew expansion, mobile-first route management, before/after photo documentation (critical for proof of service), and offline capability for rural and commercial job sites.

## Industry Profile

### Market Size
- U.S. pressure washing services: **$1.2 billion** (2024 revenue)
- **32,193 pressure washing businesses** in the United States
- Approximately **41,799 technicians** employed across the industry
- Global pressure washer market: **$3.24-$5.46 billion** (2025, varies by source)
- Industry growing at average of **5.7% per year** (2018-2024)
- Highly fragmented -- dominated by small local operators

### Industry Segments
- **Solo operator (majority):** 1 person, truck + trailer, $20K-$50K revenue (part-time) or $55K-$100K (full-time)
- **Small company:** 2-5 crew members, 1-2 rigs, $100K-$300K revenue
- **Established operation:** 5-15 crew, multiple rigs, $300K-$1M+ revenue
- **Multi-crew commercial:** 15+ employees, fleet of rigs, $1M-$5M+ revenue
- **Residential vs. commercial split:** Long-term commercial contracts represent **58% of the market**
- **61% of commercial clients** opt for recurring scheduled cleaning arrangements

### Service Pricing
- Average residential job: **$150-$600** (driveway, house wash, deck)
- Pricing per square foot: **$0.30-$0.80** (varies by surface type and condition)
- Driveway: $0.50/sq ft | Patio/deck: $0.55/sq ft | Siding: $0.50/sq ft | Roof: $0.70/sq ft
- Target average ticket for profitable operations: **$800-$1,200** (residential)
- Successful operators maintain average tickets exceeding **$1,400**
- Profit margins: **40-50%** depending on equipment, insurance, and demand

### Seasonal Revenue Pattern
- **Peak season (March-October):** 70-85% of annual revenue in most climates
- **Southern states:** Near year-round operation (reduced winter but not zero)
- **Northern states:** 3-4 month winter dead zone (November-February)
- Maintenance plans and bundled follow-ups softening seasonality
- Smart operators offer complementary off-season services (Christmas lighting, gutter cleaning, window washing)

### Technology Adoption
- Most small operators use phone, text, and paper estimates
- Digital platforms entering the market but no clear winner in the segment
- Customer expectations shifting toward online booking and instant quoting
- Before/after photo documentation increasingly expected by commercial clients
- Route optimization software can save 45+ minutes per day per crew
- Satellite property measurement tools (CrewNest) emerging for remote quoting

### Labor Situation
- **43% of operators report labor shortages** as a major challenge
- **46% cite rising operational costs**
- **34% struggle with advanced equipment investment**
- Seasonal hiring is standard -- crew sizes double or triple during peak season
- Per-user software pricing directly conflicts with seasonal hiring model
- Many crews are Spanish-speaking -- bilingual software support matters
