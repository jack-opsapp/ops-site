- **Headquarters:** Dallas, TX
- **CEO Background:** Gabriel Pinchev attended Southern Methodist University - Cox School of Business. Started FieldPulse after seeing contractors drowning in handwritten paperwork and not getting paid.
- **Notable:** Named to 2025 Inc. 5000 list of fastest-growing private companies

### Pricing (Verified as Hidden)
- **Published price:** None. FieldPulse does NOT publish pricing on their website. All quotes require contacting sales.
- **Pricing page:** fieldpulse.com/pricing exists but shows no numbers -- just a "Book a Free Demo" CTA.
- **Third-party estimates (from contractor-reported data):**
  - Base plan: ~$99/month (1 user)
  - Essentials: ~$189/month (1-5 users)
  - MAX: ~$329/month (up to 8 users)
  - Additional users: ~$30/user/month beyond plan cap
  - Rough range: $99-$399/month depending on team size and tier
- **Add-on costs (verified):**
  - Engage VoIP (business phone system): additional cost (amount not published)
  - Fleet tracking (GPS): $30/vehicle/month ($150/month for a 5-truck fleet = $1,800/year)
  - Payment processing: 2.9% per transaction
  - Operator AI (AI dispatcher): additional cost (amount not published)
- **Total estimated cost for 5-person team:** $600-$800+/month once add-ons are factored in
- **Contract requirements:** All customers signed up after November 2023 are on annual contracts. No month-to-month option.
- **Cancellation:** Requires 60-day notice before end of annual billing period. Cannot cancel mid-term. Must fulfill entire contract obligation. Email cancellations@fieldpulse.com.
- **Free trial:** 14 days, no credit card required
- **Annual billing discount:** 10-20% off monthly rates (reported)

### Primary Market
- **Core trades:** HVAC, plumbing, electrical (primary development priorities aligned to these three)
- **Extended trades:** Garage door, appliance repair, locksmith, septic, roofing, glass repair, pool service, property management, landscaping, pest control, AV installation
- **Company sizes:** Small to mid-size service businesses, typically 2-15 technicians
- **Customer profile:** Growing businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets/paper but aren't ready for enterprise platforms like ServiceTitan or BuildOps
- **Both commercial and residential** -- unlike BuildOps (commercial-only)

### Known Strengths (Be Honest)
- **Customer support is genuinely excellent:** Award-winning Customer Success team. Hands-on onboarding with dedicated implementation specialists. Reviewers consistently praise the responsiveness of support staff. "Not just answering questions but sometimes taking care of issues directly."
- **Feature breadth for mid-market:** Scheduling, CRM, estimates, invoicing, payments, inventory, project management, pricebook, fleet tracking, maintenance agreements, franchise management -- all in one platform.
- **Customizable workflows:** More flexible than Jobber for complex job types (multi-phase, commercial projects). "FieldPulse earns its premium over Jobber when jobs don't fit the 'schedule, complete, invoice' model."
- **Operator AI:** 24/7 AI dispatcher that answers after-hours/overflow calls and books jobs directly into the system. Competitive differentiator.
- **Drag-and-drop dispatch board:** Color-coded job statuses, calendar view, easy to read.
- **Rapid product development:** Series C funding ($50M) dedicated to AI innovation, platform customization, and customer success expansion.
- **Financial processing built in:** Invoicing, payments, profit margin tracking with markup rules per job.
- **Franchise management capability:** Multi-location support.

### Key Weaknesses
- **Pricing is completely hidden:** Must contact sales for any pricing information. This is the #1 frustration cited by prospects evaluating the platform. "FieldPulse does not publicly disclose pricing on their website, and to get a quote, you must contact their sales team -- a friction point that wastes time and often leads to pressure tactics."
- **Add-on cost creep:** Core features like VoIP calling, texting, GPS tracking, and AI are all paid add-ons. One reviewer: "Marketing makes the software seem top-tier but doesn't disclose you need add-ons for basic functionality like calling, texting, and emailing. I spent over $1,000 in add-ons."
- **Offline mode is unreliable:** Despite being advertised, users consistently report data loss in areas with poor cell coverage (basements, rural properties, inside commercial buildings). - **QuickBooks integration problems:** Desktop sync creates duplicate entries, fails to sync consistently, requires manual intervention. Multiple reviewers cite QB Desktop sync as their #1 frustration.
- **Engage VoIP tool is unstable:** "The Engage tool feels unstable and limited; the inability to forward calls to multiple people is a major bottleneck." Another: "The engage add on was a complete waste of time and money. I created a phone number and got it set up. I asked a few people to call it to test. 50% of them their phone just hung up and did not ring."
- **Bugs and unfinished product feel:** "We ultimately feel like we are working with an unfinished product that is still being beta tested in a lot of ways." And: "There are quite a few bugs or shortcomings in the programming."
- **Annual contract lock-in:** All post-November 2023 customers on annual contracts with 60-day cancellation notice. Cannot exit mid-term.
- **Price increases without warning:** Multiple users report unexpected price increases after the first year.
- **Cluttered navigation:** "Nested menus and similar labels across modules can slow navigation." "Some of the repetitive/similar terminology makes it hard to differentiate."
- **No intelligent dispatching:** Manual dispatching without AI-powered tech assignment or automated conflict detection.
- **Filters don't persist:** "When using filters they don't always save, and depending on the section, users have to keep re-applying filters when going in and out of files."
- **Virtually zero Reddit presence:** Compare to Jobber and Housecall Pro which get organic advocacy on r/HVAC, r/Plumbing, r/electricians, r/sweatystartup. FieldPulse has almost no organic community discussion.
- **Claims "#1 FSM Software" with no evidence:** Marketing claim on website without any verifiable backing.

## Pain Points (for 3 painPoint blocks)

### Pain Point 1: "The Price? You'll Have to Call to Find Out."

**Bullet material:**
- FieldPulse does not publish any pricing on their website. The pricing page exists (fieldpulse.com/pricing) but contains no numbers -- just a "Book a Free Demo" button.
- To learn what FieldPulse costs, you must contact their sales team, book a demo, and receive a "custom quote." This friction point wastes contractor time and enables pressure tactics.
- Third-party sources estimate $99-$399/month depending on team size, but the real costs stack up with mandatory add-ons: GPS tracking at $30/vehicle/month, VoIP phone system, payment processing at 2.9%, and AI features all cost extra.
- One reviewer stated: "Marketing makes the software seem top-tier but doesn't disclose you need add-ons for basic functionality like calling, texting, and emailing. I spent over $1,000 in add-ons."
- A 5-person crew with 5 vehicles could easily pay $600-$800+/month once per-user fees, GPS tracking ($150/month), and other add-ons are factored in.
- All customers since November 2023 are locked into annual contracts. 60-day cancellation notice required. Cannot cancel mid-term.
- Multiple users report unexpected price increases after the first year without adequate notice.
- For context: Jobber publishes pricing at $39-$349/month. Housecall Pro publishes at $59-$189/month. OPS is free to start.

**Target audience line:** Small and mid-size contractors (2-15 technicians) who have been burned by hidden pricing, surprise add-on costs, or annual contract lock-in.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "Marketing makes the software seem top-tier but doesn't disclose you need add-ons for basic functionality like calling, texting, and emailing. I spent over $1,000 in add-ons." (Capterra)
- "The lack of pricing transparency makes it hard to budget, and several users report unexpected price increases after the first year." (Review aggregator)
- "Manual dispatching burning hours, add-on costs creeping up, offline mode losing data, or you're just tired of not knowing what your bill will be next month." (Review aggregator)
- "Also, it is pricey. Has a lot of functionality but it comes with a steep price tag per user." (Capterra/G2)

### Pain Point 2: "Offline Mode That Doesn't Actually Work Offline"

**Bullet material:**
- FieldPulse advertises offline mode as a feature. In practice, users consistently report it is unreliable and causes data loss.
- Field service technicians routinely work in environments with poor cell coverage: basements, crawl spaces, rural properties, inside commercial buildings with thick walls. This is where offline mode matters most -- and where FieldPulse fails.
- One reviewer stated directly: "It is supposed to be offline compatible. But it is not. If I have no service I am not getting into the system at all."
- The mobile app compounds this problem: Google Play rating of 3.2/5, iOS App Store rating of 3.7/5. Users report a cramped interface, too many taps to complete basic actions, and photo upload bugs.
- Multiple reviewers describe the mobile app as "not user-friendly" for new users and report needing to "delete the app and reinstall" to resolve issues -- hardly practical for a technician mid-job.
- "We ultimately feel like we are working with an unfinished product that is still being beta tested in a lot of ways."
- For field crews wearing gloves, working in the heat or rain, needing to quickly log a job note or check their schedule, an unreliable mobile app is not a minor inconvenience -- it is a dealbreaker.

**Target audience line:** Field technicians and crews who work in low-connectivity environments (basements, rural areas, commercial buildings) and need an app that actually works without cell service.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "It is supposed to be offline compatible. But it is not. If I have no service I am not getting into the system at all." (Capterra/G2)
- "We ultimately feel like we are working with an unfinished product that is still being beta tested in a lot of ways." (Capterra)
- "There are quite a few bugs or shortcomings in the programming." (Capterra)
- "If issues arise, deleting the app and reinstalling is almost always necessary, and it is very inconvenient most times." (Review aggregator)

### Pain Point 3: "QuickBooks Sync That Creates More Work Than It Saves"

**Bullet material:**
- QuickBooks Desktop integration is cited as the #1 integration frustration by FieldPulse users across Capterra and G2.
- The web connector integration creates duplicate entries, fails to sync consistently, and requires manual intervention to clean up.
- FieldPulse's own help center has extensive troubleshooting documentation for sync failures -- Pre-Sync, Sync, and Post-Sync guides for both QuickBooks Desktop and Online -- acknowledging the complexity of getting it right.
- Known sync failure causes include: duplicate line item names, archived customers not syncing, and users creating estimates/invoices in both QuickBooks and FieldPulse before syncing (duplicating effort).
- The Engage VoIP add-on has its own integration problems: "The engage add on was a complete waste of time and money. I created a phone number and got it set up. I asked a few people to call it to test. 50% of them their phone just hung up and did not ring."
- After experiencing integration failures, one user tried to cancel the Engage add-on: "After multiple attempts they never fixed the issue and tried to deny my request for a refund of engage when I was canceling."
- Beyond QuickBooks, users report that moving between estimates, jobs, and invoices "still feels a bit disjointed, with information between these categories needing engineering work to avoid constantly copying and pasting."

**Target audience line:** Contractors who rely on QuickBooks for accounting and need their field service software to sync cleanly without creating duplicates, missing entries, or hours of manual reconciliation.

**Supporting quotes:**

## FAQ Material (5 questions)

### Q1: "How much does FieldPulse actually cost?"
FieldPulse does not publish pricing on their website. All quotes require contacting their sales team and booking a demo. Third-party sources estimate $99-$399/month depending on team size, but the real cost is higher once you factor in mandatory add-ons: GPS tracking at $30/vehicle/month, VoIP phone system, payment processing at 2.9%, and AI features all carry additional charges. One user reported spending over $1,000 on add-ons for what they described as "basic functionality." All customers since November 2023 are on annual contracts with 60-day cancellation requirements. OPS publishes pricing upfront with no hidden add-on costs.

### Q2: "Does FieldPulse work offline?"
FieldPulse advertises offline mode, but user reviews consistently report it is unreliable. Technicians working in basements, rural areas, or inside commercial buildings with poor cell coverage report being unable to access the system at all. One reviewer stated: "It is supposed to be offline compatible. But it is not. If I have no service I am not getting into the system at all." For field crews who regularly work in low-connectivity environments, this is a critical limitation.

### Q3: "Is FieldPulse good for small contractors?"
FieldPulse targets small to mid-size service businesses with 2-15 technicians, and its feature set is comprehensive. However, the hidden pricing, annual contract requirements, and add-on cost structure can make it expensive for small teams. A 5-person crew with vehicles could pay $600-$800+/month. If you need a simpler, more affordable platform that does not require a sales call to learn the price, OPS is free to start with no credit card required.

### Q4: "Does FieldPulse integrate with QuickBooks?"
Yes, FieldPulse integrates with both QuickBooks Desktop (2023+) and QuickBooks Online. However, the QuickBooks Desktop integration is the most-complained-about feature across review platforms. Users report duplicate entries, sync failures, and the need for manual intervention to clean up accounting records. FieldPulse has extensive troubleshooting documentation for sync issues, which itself suggests the problem is widespread.

### Q5: "What is the best FieldPulse alternative in 2026?"
The most commonly cited FieldPulse alternatives are Jobber, Housecall Pro, and ServiceTitan. However, these platforms each have their own limitations: Jobber gates features behind expensive tiers, Housecall Pro has collapsed customer support to AI-only, and ServiceTitan costs $250-$500/user/month with multi-month implementations. OPS is purpose-built for field crews with transparent pricing, reliable offline mode, and a mobile-first interface designed for gloved hands -- not desktop dashboards.
