#### Typical HVAC Company Profile

- **Small operators (1-3 trucks):** $150K-$500K annual revenue. Owner-operator with 1-4 employees. This is the majority of the market.
- **Mid-sized (5-20 employees):** $1M-$5M annual revenue. Dedicated dispatcher, 2-6 crews in the field, admin staff of 1-3.
- **Large enterprises (50+ employees):** $10M-$50M+ annual revenue. Multiple locations, dedicated IT, procurement, HR. ServiceTitan's actual target.
- **Most HVAC businesses are small, family-owned operations with fewer than 5 employees and annual revenues under $1 million.** This is OPS's primary audience.

#### Residential vs. Commercial Split

- Residential HVAC accounts for 52-70% of total market revenue (depending on measurement methodology).
- Commercial HVAC accounts for 30-48%.
- Residential service calls have 35-50% profit margins vs. 12-20% for commercial installs.
- Maintenance agreements account for 39% of industry revenue and growing at 8.3% CAGR.

#### FSM Adoption Rates

- **56% of HVAC contractors have field service management software** (but most underutilize it).
- **65%+ use at least one digital scheduling/dispatch tool** (includes Google Calendar, spreadsheets).
- **58% use mobile-based job management tools.**
- **47% use real-time technician tracking.**
- **Cloud-based deployments account for 62%+ of active installations.**
- **The remaining 35-44% still rely on pen-and-paper, whiteboards, spreadsheets, or basic calendars.**

#### Key Workflow Characteristics Unique to HVAC

1. **Seasonal demand swings:** Peak demand July-October, second peak in winter heating season. October (not mid-summer) is actually the busiest month. Spring/fall are slow seasons where techs are underbooked. Smart contractors shift maintenance visits to slow months to "flatten the curve."
2. **Emergency dispatch / after-hours calls:** The average HVAC company misses 23% of inbound calls during business hours, jumping to 62% after hours -- representing $180,000+ in lost annual revenue for shops running 50 calls/week.
3. **Preventive maintenance contracts:** 39% of all HVAC revenue comes from maintenance contracts. Fewer than 35% of residential HVAC companies actively sell them. 90% of maintenance revenue recurs year over year. Techs should convert 25-50% of service calls into agreements.
4. **EPA compliance / refrigerant tracking:** Section 608 requires documentation of every refrigerant transaction for systems with 50+ lbs. Records must be retained 3+ years. Fines start at $44,539/day/violation. Manual calculation errors are the #1 source of compliance failures.
5. **Workforce shortage:** 110,000 unfilled technician positions. 25,000 leave the workforce annually. 40% of HVAC techs are over 45. 225,000 vacant positions projected within 5 years. Companies that provide better tools attract and retain younger techs.
6. **Work environment:** Basements (no signal), attics (extreme heat), rooftops, crawl spaces, mechanical rooms. Techs wear gloves, deal with sunlight glare, condensation on screens, and dirty hands.

### 3. PAIN POINTS (3 Blocks, 4 Bullets Each)

#### Pain Point 1: Scheduling Chaos -- When the Heat Hits, Everything Breaks

**forLine:** For HVAC owners and dispatchers drowning in summer call volume

- **Seasonal demand creates impossible scheduling pressure.** HVAC call volume can spike 300%+ when the first heat wave hits. Dispatchers go from managing 15 calls/day to 50+ overnight. Manual scheduling systems (whiteboards, spreadsheets, even basic software) collapse under the pressure -- double-bookings, missed appointments, and angry callbacks become daily events.
- **Emergency calls blow up the schedule.** An AC failure in a 95-degree home is an emergency. But fitting emergency calls into an already packed schedule means bumping existing appointments, which creates a cascading chain of reschedules, customer complaints, and lost revenue. "Scheduling errors are rampant -- overlapping bookings, double-scheduling, and missing work orders plague dispatchers" (BuildOps).
- **After-hours calls are a black hole.** 62% of after-hours calls go unanswered, representing $180K+ in annual lost revenue per shop. Most scheduling tools have no after-hours dispatch workflow -- the tech on call gets a text, maybe, and figures it out from there.
- **The slow season is wasted.** Spring and fall see idle technicians while summer and winter are chaos. Fewer than 35% of residential HVAC companies actively sell maintenance agreements that could smooth demand across the year. The software most use does not help them build or manage these recurring revenue streams.

#### Pain Point 2: The Office-Field Gap -- Your Dispatcher and Your Tech Are in Different Worlds

**forLine:** For HVAC techs and dispatchers tired of phone tag and missed updates

- **Field techs get half the picture.** A tech arrives at a job site without knowing the equipment history, the customer's previous complaints, or that the dispatcher changed the job scope 20 minutes ago. "Field technicians often experience the ripple effects: more calls back to the office, more interruptions for dispatchers, and slower job completion overall" (MIS Solutions).
- **Phone tag replaces real communication.** Instead of one app everyone looks at, the office calls the tech, the tech calls back from a noisy rooftop, the message gets garbled, and the wrong part gets pulled from the truck. "Poor communication between office staff and technicians can lead to mistakes, such as technicians arriving at the wrong locations or times, causing delays and frustration" (Jobber).
- **Signal dead zones kill productivity.** HVAC techs regularly work in basements, crawl spaces, mechanical rooms, and attics -- all notorious for zero cell signal. If the app requires constant connectivity, the tech cannot update job status, view work orders, or capture photos until they climb back out. Most apps fail here.
- **Notes and photos vanish.** Techs take notes on a clipboard, snap photos on their personal phone, and promise to enter it all when they get back to the truck. Half the time, it never makes it into the system. "Users report issues with the app when taking notes, capturing images, or filling out forms -- instances of lost time when they won't upload or sync successfully" (Capterra / Jobber reviews).

**Supporting quotes:**
