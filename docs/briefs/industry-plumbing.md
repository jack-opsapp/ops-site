#### Key Workflow Characteristics Unique to Plumbing

1. **Emergency/same-day dispatch is the norm, not the exception.** A morning of scheduled drain cleanings gets disrupted by an emergency water main break. Plumbing dispatchers must slot emergency calls into packed schedules on the fly -- a capability that generic calendar apps cannot handle.

2. **Parts inventory on trucks is critical.** Pipes, fittings, water heaters, and specialty valves must be stocked on service trucks. Holding the right inventory reduces costly re-visits. The industry recommends weekly truck stock reviews and per-technician reorder points.

3. **Residential vs. commercial split.** Residential drives high volume of service calls; commercial/institutional delivers higher per-project revenue. Most small plumbing companies do both, creating scheduling complexity (a 30-minute faucet repair followed by a 3-day commercial rough-in).

4. **Water heater replacement dominates revenue.** Water heater installation represents ~70% of water heating business revenue, vs. 17% repair and 13% maintenance. This high-ticket replacement work requires on-site estimation capability.

5. **Service agreements are the growth lever.** Maintenance contracts (annual inspections, drain cleaning, water heater flushes) generate recurring revenue, reduce emergency calls, and provide a "foot in the door" for upselling.

6. **Licensing requirements vary by state.** All 50 states require some form of plumbing license. Renewals are annual, biennial, or triennial with continuing education requirements. Multi-state operations face significant compliance complexity.

7. **Technician utilization is low.** Average plumber logs only 2.4 billable hours per 8-hour day (30% utilization). Technicians spend 28% of their day driving. Every scheduling efficiency gain directly impacts revenue.

8. **Job volume: ~3.5 billable jobs per truck per day** at 250 working days/year. A 5-truck operation handles 17-18 jobs daily -- enough to overwhelm a whiteboard dispatcher but not enough to justify $50K in software.

### 3. PAIN POINTS (3 Blocks, 4 Bullets Each)

#### Pain Point 1: DISPATCH CHAOS -- Emergency Calls Destroy the Schedule

**forLine:** For plumbing dispatchers juggling emergencies and scheduled jobs

- **Emergency calls wreck the entire day.** A morning of scheduled drain cleanings gets blown apart by one burst pipe call. Manual dispatch systems have no mechanism to reassign or rebalance -- the dispatcher must call every technician individually, hope they answer, and verbally rearrange the day.
- **Double-booking is rampant without real-time visibility.** Jobber's own users report that it allows double-booking with no alerts. Without live schedule views, dispatchers accidentally send two techs to the same address while another job goes unserved. One plumber on Plumbing Zone: "any good dispatch/scheduling software?" -- the search itself reveals the unmet need.
- **$15,000/month walks out the door from missed calls.** Plumbing businesses miss 27-40% of incoming calls. Only 5% of callers leave voicemails; the other 95% call the next plumber. Each missed call represents $500-$1,200 in potential work. Emergency jobs command 1.5-2x premium pricing.
- **Manual scheduling caps out at ~10 jobs/day.** Paper calendars, whiteboards, and spreadsheets work for a solo operator but collapse once you have 2-3 trucks. Beyond that threshold, the complexity of juggling technician availability, emergency calls, travel time, and customer preferences exceeds human capacity.
