## Pain Points (for 3 painPoint blocks)

### Pain Point 1: "Generic Software for Every Trade Means Purpose-Built for None"

**Bullet material:**
- Zuper markets to HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, solar, pool maintenance, landscaping, cleaning, security, ISPs, facility maintenance, manufacturing, construction, and more. All from one generic platform with no trade-specific workflows.
- Their homepage says "All in One Field Service Management Software" -- the definition of generic horizontal positioning. No trade-specific language, no trade-specific features, no trade-specific onboarding.
- A roofer, a pool tech, and an HVAC installer all get the same screens, the same workflows, the same configuration nightmare. None of them feel like Zuper was built for their trade.
- Zuper requires extensive customization to fit any specific trade workflow. Users report "an overwhelming amount of configuration options" that require weeks of training and support to configure properly.
- The learning curve exists BECAUSE the product is generic. When software is built for your trade, it works out of the box. When it is built for every trade, you spend 12 weeks making it work.
- The estimate platform is described as "pretty basic" because no single estimating workflow can serve roofers, electricians, and pool techs equally.
- Custom reporting must be requested through the Zuper team because the generic report templates do not fit any specific trade's KPIs.

**Target audience line:** Trade contractors (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing) who tried Zuper and realized the "all-in-one for everyone" pitch means "built for no one in particular."

**Supporting quotes:**
- "The software has a steep learning curve and the UI is a bit outdated and clunky." (G2)
- "The system doesn't provide much room for customization and isn't very intuitive." (Review platform)
- "Certain features are less developed than comparable FSM solutions, with custom reporting needing to be requested through the Zuper team." (Capterra)
- "The estimate platform is poor with very limited functionality and is inferior to most other products." (Review platform)

### Pain Point 2: "The Mobile App Your Crew Will Hate"

**Bullet material:**
- Zuper's main mobile app (Zuper - Field Service App) has a 3.0-star rating on Google Play. Their Pro app has 3.9 stars. Both are well below what field crews need from a tool they use all day.
- Users report the app crashes in the middle of tasks. "The app is unstable and crashes in the middle of certain tasks, even after daily updates."
- "Too many clicks required" for simple operations. Field crews wearing gloves, standing on a roof, or working in a ditch do not have time for 4-tap workflows.
- "The app likes to disappear while in the middle of typing." When you are capturing job notes on-site, losing your work mid-sentence is unacceptable.
- Mobile app synchronization delays cause data inconsistencies between field and office.
- Offline mode described as "spotty" -- a critical failure for crews working in basements, rural areas, or inside buildings.
- "A completely unintuitive user interface has led users to spending extra time and resources to train staff in the workings of the system."
- Navigation and search on mobile "aren't always smooth, which can make daily tasks less efficient."
- For a company that raised $46.1M in funding and has 298 employees, a 3.0-star mobile app is a product failure.

**Target audience line:** Field crews and foremen who need a mobile app that works reliably on the job site -- not one that crashes, lags, and requires too many taps.

**Supporting quotes:**
- "The app is not the best, with too many clicks required and instability issues, including crashes in the middle of tasks." (App Store review)
- "While the overall idea is great, there's a steep learning curve that makes it more frustrating than helpful." (Google Play review)
- "Navigation and search aren't always smooth, which can make daily tasks less efficient." (Review platform)
- "Offline mode for the app can be spotty." (Review aggregator)

## FAQ Material (5 questions)

### Q1: "Is Zuper good for small contractors?"
Zuper is designed for mid-market and enterprise field service organizations. While they accept smaller customers, the 12-week implementation timeline, hidden pricing, and steep learning curve make Zuper a poor fit for small crews that need software working this week. The platform requires extensive customization to fit any specific trade, and custom reporting must be requested through their team. For small contractors (under 20 employees), OPS is free to start and works out of the box without weeks of configuration.

### Q2: "How much does Zuper cost?"
Zuper does not publish pricing. You must contact their sales team for a quote. Third-party sources report starting prices of $35-$65/user/month. For a 10-person crew, that works out to approximately $4,200-$7,800/year before any implementation or training costs. Zuper offers three tiers (Starter, Growth, Enterprise) but the specific price for each tier is hidden behind a sales conversation.

### Q3: "Does Zuper have a good mobile app?"
Zuper's mobile experience is a documented weakness. The main Zuper Field Service app has a 3.0-star rating on Google Play. The Zuper Pro app rates slightly better at 3.9 stars. Users report crashes during tasks, too many clicks for simple operations, spotty offline mode, and synchronization delays. For field crews who rely on their phone as their primary work tool, a 3.0-star app is a liability.

### Q4: "How long does Zuper implementation take?"
Full Zuper implementation can take up to 12 weeks. Users report that the platform has a steep learning curve with a non-intuitive interface that requires extensive training. Some users report working with Zuper for close to a year with issues still unresolved. The platform's generic design means significant configuration is needed to fit any specific trade's workflow.

### Q5: "Is Zuper built for my specific trade?"
Zuper is a generic, horizontal FSM platform that markets to every trade -- HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, solar, pool, landscaping, cleaning, manufacturing, ISPs, and more. This means no trade gets purpose-built workflows. You get the same screens whether you are a roofer or an ISP technician. The extensive customization required to make Zuper fit your trade is the reason implementation takes 12 weeks and the learning curve is steep.
