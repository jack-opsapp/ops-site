## Industry Profile

**Market Size:**
- U.S. roofing contractor market: $100.5 billion (2026 estimate)
- Roofing software market: $1.5 billion (2023), projected $2.5B by 2031 at 5.9% CAGR
- 101,679 roofing contractor businesses in the U.S. (2025, up 2.7% YoY)
- No single company holds more than 5% market share -- extremely fragmented

**Typical Company Size:**
- Small residential: 5-15 employees, 2-3 crews, $500K-$3M revenue
- Mid-size residential/commercial: 15-50 employees, 3-10 crews, $3M-$15M revenue
- Typical crew: 4-5 people including foreman; 5 roofers + 1 laborer is standard for residential
- Workforce composition: 54% full-time employees, 29% subcontractors, 17% part-time
- Revenue per employee benchmark: $340K

**FSM Adoption Rate: LOW-to-MEDIUM**
- Approximately 60% have adopted "some form of CRM" -- but actual daily usage is far lower
- Many still rely on spreadsheets, text messages, whiteboards, and paper
- Main barriers: cost ($100-300/user/month for leading tools), complexity, crew resistance to technology
- A 5-person team on AccuLynx spends $19,000 over 3 years; on JobNimbus, $9,000
- Per-user pricing punishes seasonal hiring and growth

**Key Workflow Characteristics (what makes roofing unique):**
1. **Weather dependency** -- Rain, wind, and extreme temperatures halt work instantly. Entire weeks get reshuffled. Predictive scheduling around weather is critical.
2. **Multi-crew coordination** -- A mid-size roofer runs 2-5 crews simultaneously across different job sites. The office must track location, status, and materials for all crews in real time.
3. **Insurance/storm restoration workflow** -- A major segment of roofing is insurance claims work (storm damage). This involves Xactimate estimates, adjuster coordination, supplement filing, photo documentation, and multi-stage billing (ACV then RCV).
4. **Aerial measurement integration** -- Tools like EagleView and Hover provide roof measurements from aerial imagery. These integrate with CRMs but not with most general FSM tools.
5. **Material ordering and waste** -- Roofing materials (shingles, underlayment, flashing) must be ordered per-job. Over-ordering creates waste; under-ordering causes delays. Supplier integrations (ABC Supply, Beacon, SRS) are roofing-specific features.
6. **Photo documentation** -- Before/during/after photos are required for insurance claims, warranty work, and customer trust. GPS-tagged, timestamped photos organized by job are table stakes.
7. **Daily morning huddle** -- The standard practice is a 10-minute morning briefing: confirm schedules, flag weather or material delays, and ensure every crew knows where they're going.
8. **High safety stakes** -- Roofing has a 57.5 fatality rate per 100K workers (second-highest in U.S.). Safety documentation, OSHA compliance, and daily safety briefings are non-negotiable.

## Pain Points (for 3 painPoint blocks)

### Pain Point 1: Scheduling Chaos and Weather Roulette

**Bullet material:**
- Weather cancels or delays jobs with zero warning -- an entire week's schedule can collapse from two days of rain, forcing every crew to be reshuffled
- Double-booking crews because the office lacks real-time visibility into who is where and what stage each job is at
- Pulling crews mid-job to start a new project breaks momentum and delays both jobs -- "Finishing two jobs late is better than starting two and delaying both"
- No buffer capacity in the schedule for weather delays, emergency callbacks, or material hold-ups, causing a domino effect of cascading reschedules
- "Jobs overlap, crews get mixed up, and you spend your morning calling people to track where everyone is"

**Target audience line (forLine):** For roofing owners and office managers juggling 2-5 crews across weather-dependent job sites

**Supporting quotes/data:**
- "Sales doesn't communicate start dates clearly. Estimators underestimate labor time. Project managers assume crews will be available. The field gets left out of scheduling conversations." -- RoofersCoffeeShop
- "Jobs are stacking up, crews stretched thin and everyone from the field to the front office feeling like they're playing a never-ending game of Whac-A-Mole." -- industry article
- Firms using integrated tech see 30-50% efficiency gains; job batching by geography cuts travel time by 30%
- Top roofers build 1-2 buffer days per week per crew to absorb disruptions

### Pain Point 2: The Office-Field Communication Black Hole

**Bullet material:**
- Crews arrive on site without the right materials, without current job details, or at the wrong address because info was buried in a text thread
- Foremen can't get answers from the office mid-job, so they make assumptions that lead to rework and wasted materials
- Job status updates rely on phone calls and text messages that get lost, creating a "nobody told me" culture
- The office invoices incomplete jobs or contacts customers with wrong status updates because they have no real-time field visibility
- Language barriers compound the problem -- the fastest-growing segment of field workers is Latino, yet most communication tools don't support bilingual crews

**Target audience line (forLine):** For roofing foremen and office coordinators tired of playing phone tag all day

**Supporting quotes/data:**
- "There is a tendency for the office to only call the field when they want something or there is a problem. And vice versa." This creates "an environment of hostility, not cooperation." -- Roofing Contractor Magazine
- Poor communication and bad data cost the U.S. construction industry over $31 billion in rework annually -- FMI/PlanGrid study
- "For every $1 spent in a pre-job meeting, there was a $4 to $5 return on investment." -- industry research
- Construction workers frequently view documentation as "punishment or busy work" rather than a necessary process -- Roofing Contractor Magazine
- The 10-minute morning huddle eliminates the majority of "nobody told me" problems, but most companies still rely on scattered texts

### Pain Point 3: Software That Costs More Than a New Crew Member

**Bullet material:**
- Leading roofing CRMs cost $100-300/user/month -- for a 10-person company, that's $12,000-$36,000/year just on software before you've paid for a single bundle of shingles
- Per-user pricing penalizes seasonal hiring: adding 3 temporary crew members for storm season costs another $300-900/month
- Implementation takes weeks or months -- JobNimbus users report 3 weeks of configuration; ServiceTitan takes 6-12 months and charges $5,000-$50,000+ in setup fees
- Tools are built for the office, not the field: "limited mobile app" and "poor reporting" are the top AccuLynx complaints; JobNimbus mobile performance is flagged repeatedly
- The software that's cheap enough is too basic; the software that's powerful enough is too expensive -- there's no middle ground for a roofing company doing $1-3M

## FAQ Material (for 5 FAQ blocks)

### Q1: What is the best scheduling app for roofing contractors?

**Answer material:**
- The best scheduling app depends on your company size and what you actually need. For large operations (20+ crews), platforms like ServiceTitan offer AI dispatch and complex routing. For insurance-heavy restoration work, AccuLynx integrates Xactimate and supplier ordering. For CRM and pipeline management, JobNimbus is popular.
- But most roofing companies (2-5 crews) don't need enterprise CRM -- they need a simple, reliable way to assign crews to jobs, communicate changes in real time, and keep the schedule visible to everyone.
- OPS fills that gap: it's a scheduling and crew management app built for the field, not the office. No implementation period, no per-user fees that punish growth, and a mobile experience designed for workers wearing gloves on a roof.
- Reference the stat: firms using integrated scheduling tech see 30-50% efficiency gains

### Q2: How do roofing companies handle weather delays in their schedule?

**Answer material:**
- Weather is the single biggest scheduling variable in roofing. Rain, high winds (typically 25+ mph), and extreme temperatures can shut down a job site instantly.
- Best practice: build 1-2 buffer days per week into each crew's schedule. Batch similar jobs by geography to reduce travel time (saves up to 30%). Use weather forecast integration to proactively reschedule before the morning of.
- Most roofing companies still handle this reactively -- the owner checks the forecast at 5 AM and starts making phone calls. Modern scheduling tools can flag at-risk days and suggest alternatives.
- OPS approach: real-time schedule visibility means when a foreman flags a weather delay from the field, the office and other crews see it immediately. Rescheduling is drag-and-drop, not a chain of phone calls.

### Q3: How much does roofing contractor software cost?

**Answer material:**
- Roofing software prices vary dramatically:
  - AccuLynx: $60-120/user/month + $500-$5K setup fee + 12-month contract
  - JobNimbus: $49-249/month (contact for exact pricing, not published)
  - ServiceTitan: ~$245/tech/month + $5K-50K implementation + 12-month contract with $15K-46K exit fees
  - Roofr: Free for measurements, $89-149/month for CRM
  - OPS: Free to start, no credit card, no sales call
- For a 10-person roofing company, annual software costs range from $0 (OPS) to $36,000+ (ServiceTitan). That's the difference between hiring another crew member or not.
- Hidden costs to watch for: per-user fees that spike during storm season, add-on features charged separately, implementation/training fees, and contract exit penalties.

### Q4: Do I need roofing-specific software, or will a general contractor app work?

**Answer material:**
- Roofing has unique operational needs that general contractor tools don't address well: weather-dependent scheduling, multi-crew dispatch across different sites, insurance claims workflows, aerial measurement integration (EagleView/Hover), and supplier ordering (ABC Supply, Beacon, SRS).
- However, many "roofing-specific" tools are really just CRMs with roofing branding -- they're built around sales pipelines and insurance claims processing, not daily field operations.
- If your primary need is crew scheduling, job tracking, and field communication, a well-designed multi-trade app that understands field work can be more practical than a roofing CRM loaded with features you'll never use.
- OPS is built for field operations across trades, including roofing. It handles the daily reality -- scheduling crews, tracking job progress, communicating in real time -- without forcing you into a CRM pipeline you don't need.

### Q5: How do I get my roofing crews to actually use the app?

**Answer material:**
- This is the #1 question every roofing owner asks, and the answer is simple: if the app is hard to use, the crew won't use it. Period.
- Construction workers view paperwork and software as "punishment or busy work." If your app requires training sessions, extensive onboarding, or typing long notes on a phone, field adoption will be near zero.
- What works: big buttons (56dp+ touch targets for gloved hands), minimal text, clear visual design that's readable in sunlight, and offline capability for areas without signal.
- The test: can your foreman open the app on a roof, check today's schedule, update a job status, and take a photo -- all in under 30 seconds without removing their gloves? If not, the app wasn't built for them.
- OPS was designed starting from the field worker's experience, not the admin dashboard. That's why crews actually open it.
