## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

- The U.S. property maintenance market is valued at **$85-$135 billion** (depending on scope) with consistent 5-6% annual growth. Over **326,000 property management companies** operate in the US, and **80% have fewer than 10 employees**.
- Every major competitor (Property Meld, Buildium, AppFolio, Latchel) builds software **for property managers and owners**, not for the maintenance crews actually doing the work. This is the single largest gap OPS can exploit.
- Per-unit/per-door pricing ($1-$2/unit/month) punishes growing companies. Property Meld's $160/month minimum with year-long contracts and **25% one-star review rate** creates a massive "alternative" keyword opportunity.
- **Nearly one-third of the maintenance/construction workforce speaks Spanish natively**, and almost no property maintenance software offers a Spanish-language crew interface.
- Maintenance responsiveness is the **single biggest controllable factor in tenant lease renewals** — tenants whose requests are resolved within 24 hours renew at rates **15-20 percentage points higher**.

## 3. PAIN POINTS

### PAIN POINT 1: Work Order Chaos Across Multiple Properties

Property maintenance teams manage requests flowing from tenants, property managers, owners, and inspections — across dozens or hundreds of scattered properties. Without a centralized system, work orders get lost in text threads, email chains, and spreadsheet tabs.

**Proof Point 1 — Fragmented communication tools:**
> "Many property managers execute this complex, communication-heavy process using fragmented tools (emails, texts, spreadsheets), resulting in stalls, friction, and a lack of clear ownership."

**Proof Point 2 — Actively searching for solutions:**
Reddit thread from r/realestateinvesting titled "Any good apps or software for managing maintenance on multiple properties?" shows landlords actively searching, with scattered recommendations — indicating no clear winner.

**Proof Point 3 — 76% time burden:**
76% of landlords spend **40 hours each month** monitoring their properties. Much consumed by manual maintenance tracking across fragmented systems.

**Proof Point 4 — Missed repairs become expensive:**
> "Relying on spreadsheets or scattered notes leaves room for missed repairs, delayed responses, and tenant frustration, with small issues becoming expensive problems."

### PAIN POINT 2: Software Designed for Property Managers, Not Maintenance Crews

The entire property maintenance software market is built for the person behind the desk. The maintenance technician who actually does the work gets a clunky mobile afterthought.

**Proof Point 1 — Crew adoption gap:**
> "While Jobber's mobile app works well for owners and office managers, if field techs rely on it all day the experience can feel different. For contractors who depend on crew-side adoption, small delays add up."

**Proof Point 2 — Property Meld vendor hostility:**
> "This requires all users including the vendors, owners, and tenants to create accounts outside of your property management software. Expecting vendors to do this is ridiculous."
> "Does not work with vendor software, the response, fire your vendor. Tenants doesn't like it, staff doesn't like it."

**Proof Point 3 — Interfaces designed for accountants:**
> "Most [property maintenance software options] feel like they were designed by accountants in the 90s."

**Proof Point 4 — Offline failures in the field:**
> "Maintenance data accuracy declines because most systems rely on continuous connectivity. In reinforced environments such as plant basements, cold storage facilities, and utility corridors, signal instability interrupts data transmission."

### PAIN POINT 3: Per-Unit Pricing Punishes Growth

Most property maintenance software charges per unit, per door, or per property. As a maintenance company grows its portfolio, software costs scale linearly.

**Proof Point 1 — Property Meld per-unit cost:**
$1.60-$2.00 per unit/month with $160/month minimum and mandatory 12-month contract. A 500-unit portfolio costs **$800-$1,000/month** just for maintenance coordination.

**Proof Point 2 — Contract lock-in:**
> "Meld makes you sign a year contract and when their product doesn't work (as they know it won't), you have to pay a huge sum to get out of the year contract."

**Proof Point 3 — Propertyware minimums:**
$1/unit/month with a **$250/month minimum**, cost-prohibitive for operators managing fewer than 250 units.

**Proof Point 4 — Hidden scaling costs:**
> "The combined approach of service tiers plus door-count pricing gives flexibility but might be difficult to clearly understand and compare the value you're getting for the final sticker price."

## 4. COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### COMPETITOR 1: Property Meld

**Overview:** The dominant "maintenance-first" add-on for property management companies. Sits on top of AppFolio, Buildium, Rent Manager.

**Pricing:**
- $1.60/unit/month (Standard plan)
- $2.00/unit/month (OPS plan — with analytics)
- $160/month minimum
- 12-month contract required
- At 500 units: $800-$1,000/month

**What Teams Love:**
- Automated scheduling pushes jobs to approved vendors
- All calls and texts consolidated into single thread
- Real-time response time tracking
- Some teams report handling **3x more tickets** after implementation

**What Teams Hate:**

> "It is made horribly." — Capterra review
> "Beware of the 12 month contract. We started the implementation and it was clear this would not work for us."
> "During our first on-boarding meeting we realized that the product did not work as pitched."
> "Does not work with vendor software, the response, fire your vendor."

**Review Distribution (Capterra):** 38% five-star, 19% four-star, 6% three-star, 13% two-star, **25% one-star**.

**Feature Gaps:**
- No offline mode
- Forces vendors/technicians to create separate accounts
- Designed for property manager's desk, not technician's pocket
- No Spanish-language interface
- No GPS tracking of crew locations
- Recurring maintenance described as "totally bad"

### COMPETITOR 2: Buildium (Maintenance Module)

**Overview:** Full property management platform with maintenance module. Most recommended all-in-one alternative.

**Pricing:**
- Essential: $58/month
- Growth: $183/month
- Premium: $375/month
- Flat fee per plan tier (not per-unit — a pricing advantage)
- Unlimited users and document storage on all plans

**What Teams Love:**
- All-in-one: accounting, leasing, maintenance in one system
- Work order tracking with vendor assignment
- Resident portal for submitting requests
- 4.5-star rating

**What Teams Hate:**
- Terminated Vendoroo integration without notice
- Blocked self-service plan upgrades
- Maintenance module is secondary, not core
- Not designed for field use — desktop-first
- No real-time crew tracking or dispatch optimization

### Other Competitors

## 5. OPS DIFFERENTIATORS FOR PROPERTY MAINTENANCE

### Crew-First Architecture
Every competitor builds for the property manager at a desk. OPS builds for the maintenance technician in a boiler room.

- **56dp touch targets** — maintenance techs wear work gloves
- **Dark theme** — readable on phone screen in direct sunlight between buildings
- **Offline mode** — works in basements, mechanical rooms, elevator shafts, utility corridors where signal dies
- **Simple interface** — What's my next job? Where is it? What do I need to know? Done

No year-long contracts. No exit fees. No per-unit surcharges. No sales calls required.

### Offline Mode — The Mechanical Room Problem
Maintenance work happens in the worst connectivity environments: basements with concrete walls, elevator machine rooms, underground parking structures, utility tunnels, rooftop mechanical penthouses. OPS works offline and syncs when you surface.

### Spanish-Language Support
- **~33% of maintenance/construction workforce** speaks Spanish natively
- Not a single major competitor offers a Spanish-language crew interface
- A tech who can't read work orders in their primary language will make mistakes or stop using the software

### Recurring Job Scheduling
Property maintenance is inherently recurring. Competitors handle this poorly:
> "The scheduling options for recurring work orders is totally bad. The fact that you can't schedule seasonal tasks without some silly back-end procedure to duplicate the work orders every year is highly frustrating."

### Key Features Property Maintenance Crews Need
1. **Single-view schedule** — What am I doing today, across which properties?
2. **GPS-based dispatch** — Who is closest to the emergency call?
3. **Photo documentation** — Before/after for every work order
4. **Offline work order access** — Basements, mechanical rooms, rural properties
5. **Recurring task automation** — Preventive maintenance without manual re-entry
6. **Simple time tracking** — Geofenced clock-in/clock-out per property
7. **Crew-readable interface** — Large text, clear icons, dark theme, Spanish option
8. **Instant onboarding** — New hire functional in 15 minutes
